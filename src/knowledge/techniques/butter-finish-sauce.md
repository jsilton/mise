# Butter Finish (Monter au Beurre)

## What It Is

A French technique of whisking cold butter into a hot sauce at the very end of cooking to create a glossy, velvety finish. The French term is "monter au beurre" (to mount with butter). This is one of the most impactful single techniques in cooking — it elevates a home sauce to restaurant quality in seconds.

## Why It Works

Cold butter is an emulsion of fat, water, and milk solids. When whisked into a hot (but not boiling) sauce:

- The fat adds richness and rounds out sharp edges
- The water and milk proteins create a glossy sheen
- The emulsion thickens the sauce slightly without flour or cornstarch
- The rapid temperature change prevents the butter from fully melting into oil — it stays emulsified

## The Method

1. Finish cooking your sauce (reduce, season, adjust acid)
2. **Turn off the heat** (critical — boiling will break the emulsion)
3. Cut 1-2 tbsp of **cold** unsalted butter into small cubes
4. Add the butter and **whisk vigorously** until fully incorporated
5. The sauce should become noticeably glossier and slightly thicker
6. Serve immediately — the emulsion is temporary

## Critical Rules

- Butter MUST be cold (straight from the fridge)
- Heat MUST be off (or very, very low)
- Whisk vigorously — lazy stirring won't emulsify
- Serve immediately — reheating will break the emulsion
- Use unsalted butter so you control the seasoning

## Applications

### Silk Sauce (Tomato for Ravioli)

Use tomato puree/passata (not crushed), simmer with whole garlic cloves. Turn off heat, discard garlic, whisk in 1 tbsp cold butter. The result is a smooth, glossy sauce that coats delicate ravioli.

### Pan Sauce (After Searing Meat)

After deglazing with wine/stock, reduce by half, then mount with 2 tbsp cold butter off heat. Creates a restaurant-quality pan sauce in 2 minutes.

### Cream Sauces

Finish any cream-based sauce (mushroom, herb, peppercorn) with a knob of cold butter for extra body and sheen.

## Recipes That Use This

- San Marzano Tomato Sauce (Silk Sauce variation)
- Pan sauces for chicken, steak, pork chops
- Tikka masala (uses cold butter for glossy finish)
- Any French-influenced sauce
