# The Dam Method (Sushi Rolling)

## What It Is
A technique for preventing sushi filling from sliding out the back of the roll during rolling. A small ridge of rice at the top edge of the nori acts as a physical barrier to catch the ingredients.

## The Problem It Solves
When rolling maki, the filling naturally shifts upward as you roll forward. Without a dam, ingredients squeeze out the top, making messy, uneven rolls with bare spots.

## The Method
1. Lay nori on the bamboo mat, rough side up
2. Spread rice across the nori, leaving a 1-inch bare strip at the **top** edge
3. At the boundary where rice meets the bare strip, build a small ridge of rice — about 1/4 inch high, running the full width
4. Place your filling in a line across the **center** of the rice (not near the dam)
5. Roll from the bottom, tucking as you go — the filling pushes upward and catches against the rice dam
6. The bare nori strip seals against the damp rice to close the roll

## Tips
- Keep hands damp with tezu (water + splash of rice vinegar) so rice doesn't stick
- The dam should be subtle — a small ridge, not a wall
- Place filling in the center, not too close to the dam
- Roll with firm, even pressure — don't crush

## Source
Attributed to Chef Taka Sakaeda (Epicurious). Standard technique in professional sushi kitchens.

## Recipes That Use This
- Spicy Tuna Roll
- New York Crunch Roll
- Philly Tuna Roll
- Any standard maki roll
