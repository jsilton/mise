# Pasta Water Emulsification

## What It Is

The technique of using starchy pasta cooking water to create a silky, cohesive sauce that clings to pasta. This is the backbone of cacio e pepe, alfredo, aglio e olio, and most Italian pasta sauces.

## Why It Works

Pasta water contains dissolved starch from the cooking process. This starch acts as an emulsifier — it binds fat (butter, olive oil, rendered pork fat) and water into a smooth, creamy sauce instead of a greasy, broken mess.

## The Method

### Step 1: Cook Pasta Properly

- Use less water than you think — more concentrated starch
- Salt it well ("like the sea")
- Cook to 1 minute short of al dente (it finishes in the sauce)

### Step 2: Reserve the Water

- Ladle out at least 1-2 cups **before** draining
- This water is liquid gold — you can always add more, but you can't get it back

### Step 3: Build the Sauce

1. Melt your fat (butter, oil, rendered guanciale, etc.) in a wide skillet
2. Add a splash of pasta water (start with 1/2 cup)
3. Swirl or whisk to combine — it should look milky and unified
4. **Drag** pasta from the pot directly into the skillet with tongs (carrying extra starchy water)
5. Toss vigorously over medium-low heat, adding splashes of pasta water as needed

### Step 4: The Cheese (if applicable)

- **Turn heat OFF** before adding grated cheese
- Add in batches, tossing between each addition
- The residual heat + starchy water emulsify the cheese into the sauce
- If it tightens or clumps, add more pasta water and toss

## Key Principles

- The pasta finishes cooking IN the sauce — it absorbs flavor and releases starch
- Constant tossing creates the emulsion through mechanical action
- Heat control is critical — too hot and the cheese seizes, too cool and nothing melts
- The sauce should be glossy, flowing, and coat every strand

## Common Mistakes

- Draining pasta into a colander and then adding to sauce (loses the starchy water connection)
- Not reserving enough pasta water
- Adding cheese over high heat (clumps into rubber)
- Using pre-shredded cheese (anti-caking agents prevent smooth melting)

## Recipes That Use This

- Authentic Roman Alfredo (butter + Parmigiano only)
- Cacio e Pepe (pecorino + black pepper)
- Aglio e Olio (garlic + olive oil + chili flakes)
- Carbonara (guanciale + egg yolk + pecorino)
- Any butter/oil pasta sauce
