# Sushi-Zu (Sushi Vinegar Seasoning Ratio)

## What It Is
The seasoning mixture (su = vinegar, shi = to flavor) that transforms plain rice into sushi rice (shari). This is the single most important element in sushi — the word "sushi" literally refers to the vinegared rice.

## The Master Ratio (for 2 cups dry rice)
| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Rice Vinegar | 5 tbsp | High-quality (Marukan or Mizkan) |
| White Sugar | 1.5 tbsp | Reduced to let fish flavor shine |
| Fine Sea Salt | 1.5 tsp | |

## Preparation
1. Whisk vinegar, sugar, and salt until fully dissolved
2. Optionally steep a small piece of kombu in the mixture for deeper umami
3. Use immediately or store at room temperature — it keeps indefinitely

## Application: The Cut & Fan (Te-Zu)
1. Dump hot rice into a wide, non-metal bowl (wood is traditional)
2. Pour sushi-zu over the rice in a thin stream
3. **Cut** through the rice at a 45-degree angle with a paddle — separate grains, never mash or stir
4. Fan vigorously while cutting to rapidly cool
5. Continue 2-3 minutes until rice reaches body temperature (~98°F)

## Critical Rules
- Never refrigerate sushi rice — cold rice hardens and loses texture
- Keep covered with a damp towel at room temperature
- Use within 1-2 hours of preparation
- The rice should be glossy, with each grain distinct but slightly sticky

## Scaling
The ratio scales linearly:
- 1 cup rice → 2.5 tbsp vinegar, 0.75 tbsp sugar, 0.75 tsp salt
- 3 cups rice → 7.5 tbsp vinegar, 2.25 tbsp sugar, 2.25 tsp salt
