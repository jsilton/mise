# Velveting (Chinese Protein Prep)

## What It Is

A Chinese restaurant technique for pre-treating proteins (shrimp, chicken, pork) to keep them silky and tender during high-heat stir-frying. The thin cornstarch coating creates a barrier that protects the protein from direct heat.

## The Method

**Timing: 10 minutes before cooking — NOT hours in advance.**

Salting or coating proteins hours ahead cures them and makes them rubbery.

1. Pat the protein **completely bone-dry** with paper towels
2. Toss with:
   - 1 tsp Cornstarch
   - 1 tsp Neutral Oil (vegetable, canola, or peanut)
   - Pinch of Salt
   - Pinch of White Pepper
3. Let sit for 10 minutes, no more

## Cooking After Velveting

- Sear on **HIGH heat** for 45-60 seconds per side
- The protein should be just pink (shrimp) or just white (chicken) — carry-over cooking finishes the job
- **Remove from pan immediately** — don't let it sit in residual heat

## Why It Works

The cornstarch forms a thin shield that:

- Prevents direct contact with the screaming-hot pan
- Locks moisture inside the protein
- Creates a slight sheen that sauces cling to

## Common Mistakes

- Coating too far in advance (cures the protein, turns it rubbery)
- Not drying the protein first (wet surface = steaming, not searing)
- Cooking too long after velveting (the coating doesn't protect forever)

## Recipes That Use This

- Any Chinese stir-fry with shrimp, chicken, or pork
- Kung Pao, General Tso's, cashew chicken, shrimp lo mein
