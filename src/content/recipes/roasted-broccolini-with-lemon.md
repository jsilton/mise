## Chef's Note

Broccolini is not baby broccoli -- it is a hybrid of broccoli and Chinese broccoli (gai lan), which is why the stems are tender enough to eat whole. Roasting at high heat caramelizes the florets while keeping the stems snappy. Slicing the garlic thin rather than mincing prevents it from burning before the broccolini is done, and roasting lemon rounds alongside concentrates their juice into a jammy, sweet-tart condiment.

## Directions

1.  **Preheat:** Set oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2.  **Prep:** Trim the bottom 1/2 inch of broccolini stems. If any stalks are thicker than a pencil, split them lengthwise through the stem so they cook evenly.
3.  **Toss:** Spread broccolini on the sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, scatter garlic slices and red pepper flakes, and season generously with kosher salt and pepper. Tuck lemon rounds between the stalks.
4.  **Roast:** Bake for 12-15 minutes until the tips are charred and crispy and the stems are tender-crisp when pierced with a knife. The garlic should be golden, not dark brown.
5.  **Finish:** Squeeze the remaining lemon half over the hot broccolini. Finish with flaky sea salt. Serve immediately.
