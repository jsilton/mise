## Chef's Note

The key to a vibrant green curry is frying the curry paste in the thick cream layer of the coconut milk before adding anything else. Open the can without shaking -- scoop the thick cream from the top and fry the paste in that fat until it darkens and releases its oils. This blooms the aromatics and creates depth that simply dumping everything together cannot achieve. Do not substitute light coconut milk; the fat is where the flavor lives.

## Directions

1.  **Separate the Coconut Cream:** Without shaking the can, open the coconut milk and scoop the thick cream from the top into a wok or large skillet (about 1/3 cup). Reserve the remaining thinner coconut milk.

2.  **Fry the Paste:** Heat the coconut cream over medium-high heat until it begins to separate and sizzle. Add the green curry paste and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the paste darkens, becomes very fragrant, and oil pools around the edges. This step is essential.

3.  **Cook the Chicken:** Add the chicken pieces to the paste and toss to coat. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently, until the chicken is seared on the outside and coated in the paste.

4.  **Add the Coconut Milk:** Pour in the reserved coconut milk, kaffir lime leaves, and Thai bird chiles if using. Bring to a gentle simmer.

5.  **Add the Vegetables:** Stir in the eggplant, bell pepper, and bamboo shoots. Simmer for 12-15 minutes until the eggplant is tender and the chicken is cooked through (165F internal).

6.  **Season:** Stir in the fish sauce, palm sugar, and lime juice. Taste and adjust -- the curry should be a balance of rich, salty, sweet, and slightly sour.

7.  **Finish:** Remove from heat and fold in the Thai basil leaves. They will wilt in the residual heat.

8.  **Serve:** Ladle over jasmine rice. The curry should be saucy, not soupy -- it coats the rice rather than pooling on the plate.
