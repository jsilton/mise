## Chef's Note

The challenge with glazed carrots is getting the glaze to stick rather than pool at the bottom of the pan. The solution is a two-stage approach: roast the carrots first at high heat to drive off surface moisture and concentrate their natural sugars, then toss with the honey-butter glaze for a final blast. This creates a lacquered coating that caramelizes onto the surface rather than sliding off. Cut carrots into uniform batons so they cook at the same rate.

## Directions

1.  **Preheat:** Set oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2.  **First Roast:** Toss carrots with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer on the sheet -- crowding causes steaming. Roast for 20 minutes until the carrots are tender and starting to caramelize at the edges.
3.  **Make the Glaze:** While carrots roast, stir together the melted butter, honey, and thyme in a small bowl.
4.  **Glaze:** Remove the pan from the oven. Drizzle the honey-butter mixture over the carrots and toss with tongs to coat evenly.
5.  **Final Roast:** Return to the oven for 8-10 minutes until the glaze is bubbling and the carrots have a deep golden, lacquered finish.
6.  **Finish:** Sprinkle with flaky sea salt and extra thyme leaves. Serve warm.
