## Chef's Note

This is the foundational Italian-American red sauce — a 15-minute recipe that forms the backbone of stuffed shells, baked ziti, and eggplant parm. San Marzano tomatoes (grown in the volcanic soil near Mount Vesuvius) have lower acidity and a sweeter, more complex flavor than standard canned tomatoes. The key is restraint: warm the garlic in oil gently without browning it, then let the tomatoes do the work with a short simmer.

## Directions

1. **The Garlic:** Warm olive oil in a saucepan over medium-low heat. Add smashed garlic cloves and let them sizzle gently for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and barely golden — do not brown or they'll turn bitter.
2. **The Tomatoes:** Pour in the crushed San Marzano tomatoes. Add the basil sprig whole (you'll fish it out later). Season with a pinch of salt.
3. **The Simmer:** Let the sauce bubble gently for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce will darken slightly and thicken just enough to coat the back of a spoon.
4. **Finish:** Remove garlic cloves and basil sprig. Taste and adjust salt.

## Variations

**Silk Sauce (for ravioli):** Use tomato puree or passata instead of crushed tomatoes for a smoother texture. Leave garlic cloves whole and discard after simmering. Turn off heat and vigorously whisk in 1 tbsp cold unsalted butter at the very end — this creates a glossy, restaurant-quality finish.
