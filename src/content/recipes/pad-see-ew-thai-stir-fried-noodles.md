## Chef's Note

Pad See Ew (stir-fried soy sauce noodles) is a beloved Thai street food classic that relies on high-heat stir-frying to caramelize the noodles and vegetables. The key to the dish is developing **wok hei** (breath of the wok) by leaving the noodles undisturbed in a smoking hot pan to char. Traditional street vendors use fresh wide flat rice noodles (Sen Yai), but since they degrade quickly, dried wide rice sticks (XL) are an excellent home alternative. We use the **hot-soak method** on dried noodles to hydrate the core so they stay chewy without turning to mush. A quick **velveting marinade** of soy sauce, cornstarch, and oil keeps the chicken thighs tender. Finally, a touch of **white vinegar** and a finish of **ground white pepper** are crucial to balance the sweet dark soy sauce.

## Directions

1. **Noodle & Chicken Prep:** Prepare your chosen noodle option:
   - **If using Fresh Noodles (Option A):** Gently separate the noodles to prevent clumping.
   - **If using Dried Noodles (Option B):** Completely submerge the dried noodles in a large bowl of hot tap water for 15 to 25 minutes until pliable but still slightly firm in the center. Drain thoroughly, then immediately toss with 1 tbsp of neutral oil.
   - **Marinate the Chicken:** While the noodles are being prepped, toss the sliced chicken strips with 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp cornstarch, and 1 tsp neutral oil in a small bowl. Let marinate at room temperature.
2. **Mix the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the oyster sauce, regular soy sauce, sweet dark soy sauce, sugar, and white vinegar until the sugar is fully dissolved. Set aside.
3. **Sear the Chicken & Stems in Batches:** Heat a large wok or massive skillet over high heat with 2 tbsp of neutral oil until smoking. To prevent pan-crowding, sear the marinated chicken and Gai Lan stems (which take longer to cook than the leaves) in two separate batches, using 1 tbsp oil per batch. For each batch, sear undisturbed for 2 minutes to get a brown crust, then stir-fry for 1 to 2 minutes until cooked through. Transfer to a clean plate. For the final batch, right before transferring, push the ingredients to the side of the wok, add the minced garlic to the center, and stir-fry for 30 seconds until highly fragrant, then toss and transfer.
4. **Char the Noodles:** Add another 1 to 2 tbsp of oil to the smoking hot wok. Add the noodles in a flat layer. Let them sit **completely undisturbed for 30 to 45 seconds** to develop a charred crust.
5. **Steam-Sear:** Pour the sauce mixture over the noodles. If using dried noodles, splash in 1 to 2 tbsp of water. This creates a quick burst of steam to finish cooking the noodles. Toss vigorously for 1 minute to coat them in the caramelized sauce.
6. **Scramble the Eggs:** Push the noodles to one side of the wok. Crack the 3 eggs into the cleared space. Scramble them quickly with your spatula until just set, then fold them into the noodles.
7. **Wilt and Finish:** Return the cooked chicken, stems, and the raw Gai Lan leaves to the wok. Toss everything together over high heat for 60 seconds until the leaves are just wilted and bright green.
8. **Serve:** Remove from heat and stir in the ground white pepper. Portion immediately onto plates and serve with extra white pepper. Pairs perfectly with [Smashed Cucumber Salad](/mise/recipes/smashed-cucumber-salad).
