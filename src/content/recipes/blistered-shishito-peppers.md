## Chef's Note

Shishito peppers are mild and sweet with the occasional spicy surprise -- roughly one in ten packs real heat, which makes eating them a fun gamble. The technique is dead simple: screaming hot pan, dry peppers, no oil until the last second. You want aggressive blistering and char on the skin while keeping the interior barely cooked. The whole process takes under five minutes, making this the fastest side dish in your arsenal.

## Directions

1.  **Heat the Pan:** Set a cast-iron skillet over high heat for 2 minutes until it just begins to smoke. You need the pan ripping hot for proper blistering.

2.  **Blister:** Add the oil, then immediately add the shishito peppers in a single layer. Let them sit undisturbed for 60-90 seconds until the skins blister and blacken in spots. Toss once and cook another 60-90 seconds until charred on a second side.

3.  **Season:** Transfer peppers immediately to a serving plate. Squeeze lemon over the top, sprinkle with flaky salt and sesame seeds.

4.  **Serve:** Eat immediately while hot, picking them up by the stems. These do not hold well -- the texture goes soft within minutes.
