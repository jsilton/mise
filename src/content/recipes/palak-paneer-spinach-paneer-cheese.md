## Chef's Note

Palak paneer is the showstopper vegetarian curry in Indian restaurants, but it's equally at home on a weeknight table - fresh spinach is pureed into a silky green base, then enriched with cream and studded with cubes of fried paneer cheese. The contrast between the soft, melting paneer and the bright, slightly peppery spinach sauce is the whole appeal. Indian home cooks temper the spinach with bloomed spices and balance it with cream, yogurt, and a pinch of sugar. The dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) are not essential but add an aromatic, slightly bitter note that ties everything together. This is an elevated weeknight dinner that tastes restaurant-quality.

## Directions

1. **Blanch and Blend the Spinach:** If using fresh spinach, bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spinach in batches, stirring until wilted (about 1 minute per batch). Drain in a colander and squeeze out excess water. If using frozen spinach, thaw and squeeze out moisture thoroughly - excess liquid will dilute the sauce.

2. **Puree the Spinach:** Place the cooked spinach in a blender with 1/4 cup water and blend until completely smooth. The puree should be a deep, vibrant green. Set aside.

3. **Pan-Fry the Paneer:** Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a skillet over medium-high heat. When hot, add the paneer cubes in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Let each side cook for about 2 minutes until light golden-brown. Don't overcrowd or they won't brown. Transfer to a plate. The paneer won't be fully cooked - it will finish in the sauce.

4. **Make the Spice Base:** In a large skillet, heat 2 tbsp ghee over medium heat. Add the cumin and coriander seeds. Let them sizzle and pop for 30 seconds until fragrant.

5. **Build the Sauce Base:** Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and beginning to brown at the edges. Add the minced garlic and ginger, cooking for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the minced green chiles (if using) and cook for 30 seconds.

6. **Toast Ground Spices:** Add the turmeric and garam masala. Stir constantly for 30 seconds to bloom the spices in the hot oil. The kitchen should smell warm and aromatic.

7. **Add the Tomato:** Stir in the tomato puree and cook for 2-3 minutes until it darkens slightly and loses its raw taste. Add the pinch of sugar.

8. **Combine Spinach:** Pour the spinach puree into the pan and stir well. Simmer over medium heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the spinach is fully incorporated and the sauce has thickened slightly.

9. **Cream and Finish:** Add the heavy cream and Greek yogurt (or sour cream), stirring gently to combine. The sauce should turn lighter green and silky. Add the salt and the crushed kasuri methi. Taste and adjust - it should be balanced between creamy, savory, and slightly peppery. Add lemon juice if you want brightness.

10. **Add the Paneer:** Gently fold in the pan-fried paneer cubes. Simmer for 2-3 minutes so the paneer warms through and absorbs the sauce. Do not stir aggressively - paneer is delicate and will break apart.

11. **Serve:** Serve in shallow bowls over steamed basmati rice or with warm naan on the side. Each spoonful should have paneer, silky spinach sauce, and a drizzle of ghee.

**Paneer vs. Tofu:** Paneer is traditional and has a mild, slightly squeaky texture. Firm tofu can substitute for vegetarian/vegan versions but won't have the same richness.

**Cream Options:** Heavy cream creates the most luxurious sauce. Greek yogurt or sour cream work but will be tangier. For lighter versions, use half cream, half yogurt.

**Kasuri Methi:** These dried fenugreek leaves add a signature dried-herb note. If unavailable, substitute 1/2 tsp dried fenugreek powder or skip entirely - the dish is still delicious.

**Fresh vs. Frozen Spinach:** Fresh spinach is brighter and less iron-forward. Frozen works but choose unseasoned (not creamed). Drain very thoroughly.

**Make-Ahead:** Palak paneer keeps for 3-4 days in the refrigerator. Reheat gently on the stovetop - overcooking toughens the paneer. Add a splash of cream when reheating if the sauce has thickened too much.

**Spice Adjustment:** This is mildly spiced for family meals. Add more garam masala or red chili powder if you prefer more heat.
