## Chef's Note

Sweet potatoes are naturally sweet, so the trick is high heat roasting to caramelize their edges before the inside overcooks. Cutting them into wedges (not fries) means more surface area for caramelization. A brush of maple syrup in the last 10 minutes of cooking creates a lacquered, sticky-sweet glaze that concentrates the flavor. Kids recognize the maple-sugar combo and these disappear fast.

## Directions

1. **Prep:** Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. **Cut:** Cut each sweet potato into wedges about 1/2-inch thick, lengthwise (like you're cutting a log into planks). Pat dry with paper towels - moisture prevents crisping.
3. **Oil and Season:** Toss wedges with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer on the sheet, skin-side down. Do not crowd - they need space to caramelize.
4. **First Roast:** Bake for 18-20 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.
5. **Glaze:** Flip the wedges. Brush the tops with maple syrup (use a pastry brush). Sprinkle with smoked paprika if using.
6. **Finish Roast:** Return to oven for 8-10 minutes until the glaze is bubbling and caramelized. The edges should be dark brown and crispy.
7. **Serve:** Remove and finish with a pinch of flaky sea salt. Serve warm.
