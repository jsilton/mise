## Chef's Note

Gyudon is Tokyo comfort food - beef and onions simmered in a slightly sweet, deeply savory broth served over rice. The dish came from the Meiji era when beef became part of Japanese home cooking, and it became an iconic fast-food staple. The key is slicing the onions thin so they cook down to near-translucence and the beef stays tender - you want the onions to almost disappear into the broth while the beef stays delicate.

## Directions

1. **Make the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, mirin, sake, garlic, ginger, and beef stock. Set aside.

2. **Cook the Onions:** Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add sliced onions with a pinch of salt. Sauté for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften and start to caramelize at the edges. They should be golden and fragrant.

3. **Add the Beef:** Increase heat to high. Add sliced beef to the pan in a single layer. Let it sit for 1 minute without stirring - this develops a slight crust. Toss and cook another 1 minute until the beef is mostly cooked through but still slightly pink inside (it will finish cooking in the broth).

4. **Simmer:** Pour the sauce over the beef and onions. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 4-5 minutes. The sauce should reduce slightly and become glossy. Taste and adjust soy sauce or mirin if needed.

5. **Assemble:** Place warm rice in a bowl. Pour the beef and onion mixture with all its broth over the rice. Top with a soft-boiled or poached egg (if using) so the warm rice cooks it slightly.

6. **Garnish:** Sprinkle with sliced green onions and a pinch of shichimi togarashi if desired.

**Egg Note:** A soft-boiled egg (6-7 minutes) is traditional - when you break the yolk, it mixes with the hot broth and rice. Not essential, but it elevates this from quick dinner to something special.
