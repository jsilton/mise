## Chef's Note

This dish lives at the intersection of Korean japchae technique and Chinese wok fundamentals. Sweet potato noodles (dangmyeon) have a uniquely springy, glass-like chew that no other noodle replicates  -  but they turn into a gummy mass if you don't toss them in sesame oil immediately after draining. The cornstarch "velvet" on the shrimp isn't optional: it creates a thin barrier that keeps them succulent under high heat and gives the sauce something to grip. Bloom the garlic and ginger in oil with a pinch of gochugaru before the vegetables  -  it infuses the fat with a rounded heat that permeates the entire dish. For kids, serve the heat on the side or reduce/omit the spicy elements.

## Directions

1. **Velvet the Shrimp:** Toss cleaned shrimp with 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tbsp cornstarch in a bowl. Mix until evenly coated  -  the cornstarch creates a thin protective barrier that keeps shrimp succulent under high heat. Let sit for 10 minutes while you prep everything else.

2. **Cook the Noodles:** Boil sweet potato noodles for 6-7 minutes until just al dente  -  they should still have bite. Drain immediately, toss with 1 tsp toasted sesame oil to prevent sticking, and set aside. Don't skip the sesame oil  -  dangmyeon bonds to itself within seconds.

3. **Whisk the Sauce:** Combine 3 tbsp soy sauce, lime juice, honey, 1 tsp sesame oil, and fish sauce in a small bowl. Whisk until honey dissolves. Set near the stove.

4. **Sear the Shrimp:** Heat wok or heavy skillet over high heat (400-450°F) until smoking. Add 1 tbsp oil, swirl to coat. Sear shrimp in a single layer  -  about 1 minute per side until pink with charred spots but not fully curled. They'll finish in the sauce. Remove to a plate.

5. **Bloom the Aromatics:** Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the hot wok. Toss in garlic, ginger, white parts of green onions, and a pinch of gochugaru if using. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant  -  the oil should turn slightly red from the gochugaru. Don't let the garlic burn.

6. **High-Heat Vegetables:** Add julienned carrots first (they take longest)  -  toss for 2 minutes over high heat. Add snow peas and toss another 1-2 minutes. You want a snap when you bite a snow pea, not a limp string.

7. **The Marriage:** Add the noodles and seared shrimp back to the wok. Pour the sauce over everything. Use tongs to lift and toss constantly over high heat for 1-2 minutes. The residual cornstarch from the shrimp and the starch in the noodles will cause the sauce to thicken into a glossy glaze almost instantly.

8. **Finish:** Kill the heat. Fold in the green parts of the green onions, a final squeeze of lime, and a heavy sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds. Serve immediately  -  stir-fries don't wait.
