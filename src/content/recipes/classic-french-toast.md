## Chef's Note

The secret to French toast that is custardy inside without being soggy is the bread choice and the soak time. Challah and brioche have an enriched, egg-based structure that absorbs custard without disintegrating the way sandwich bread does. Day-old bread is even better because the slight staleness means it absorbs more evenly. Do not over-soak -- 20-30 seconds per side is enough for thick-cut slices. Medium-low heat is essential; too high and the outside burns before the egg mixture inside has set.

## Directions

1.  **Make the Custard:** In a wide, shallow dish (a pie plate works well), whisk together eggs, milk, cream, maple syrup, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until completely smooth. No streaks of egg white should remain.
2.  **Soak:** Dip each bread slice into the custard for 20-30 seconds per side. Let excess drip off for a few seconds. The bread should be saturated but not falling apart.
3.  **Heat the Pan:** Melt 1 tbsp butter in a large nonstick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Swirl to coat.
4.  **Cook:** Place 2-3 slices in the pan (do not crowd). Cook for 3-4 minutes until the bottom is deep golden brown. Flip and cook for another 2-3 minutes. The toast should feel slightly firm to the touch in the center -- a gentle press should spring back.
5.  **Repeat:** Wipe the pan, add fresh butter, and cook remaining slices.
6.  **Serve:** Dust with powdered sugar and serve immediately with fresh berries and warm maple syrup.
