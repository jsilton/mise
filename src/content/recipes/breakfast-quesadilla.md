## Chef's Note

A breakfast quesadilla should be crispy on the outside, melty on the inside, and ready in under 15 minutes. The key is scrambling the eggs first and slightly undercooking them -- they will continue to cook inside the quesadilla from the residual heat and the time on the griddle. Using a dry skillet over medium heat (no oil) to cook the quesadilla allows the tortilla to crisp without becoming greasy. Press gently with a spatula while cooking to ensure the cheese makes full contact with the eggs and creates a cohesive, sliceable pocket.

## Directions

1.  **Scramble Eggs:** Melt butter in a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Whisk eggs with salt and pour into the skillet. Stir gently with a spatula until the eggs are just barely set -- slightly wetter than you would normally eat them. Remove to a plate.
2.  **Build the Quesadilla:** Wipe the skillet clean. Lay one tortilla flat. Spread half the cheese on one half of the tortilla, top with half the scrambled eggs and jalapenos (if using), then add the remaining cheese on top. Fold the tortilla in half. Repeat with the second tortilla.
3.  **Cook:** Place the quesadilla in the dry skillet over medium heat. Cook for 2-3 minutes, pressing gently with a spatula, until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Flip carefully and cook for 2 minutes more until the other side is golden and the cheese is fully melted.
4.  **Slice and Serve:** Transfer to a cutting board. Let rest for 30 seconds, then cut into thirds. Serve with salsa and sour cream on the side.
