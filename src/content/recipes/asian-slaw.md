## Chef's Note

This slaw gets its crunch from a mix of green and red cabbage, and its addictive quality from the sesame dressing that balances sweet, salty, sour, and nutty in every bite. Unlike creamy coleslaws, this version stays crisp for hours -- the rice vinegar lightly pickles the cabbage rather than wilting it. Make the dressing ahead and toss just before serving for maximum crunch. This is the side that belongs next to anything with Asian flavors: grilled meats, tacos, rice bowls, or eaten straight from the bowl with chopsticks.

## Directions

1.  **Make the Dressing:** In a small bowl or jar, whisk together rice vinegar, soy sauce, sesame oil, neutral oil, honey, ginger, garlic, and sriracha (if using). The dressing can be made up to 5 days ahead and refrigerated.

2.  **Prep the Vegetables:** Thinly shred both cabbages -- a sharp knife or mandoline works best. Julienne the carrots into thin matchsticks. Thinly slice the scallions. Combine all vegetables in a large bowl.

3.  **Toss:** Pour the dressing over the vegetables and toss thoroughly, making sure the dressing reaches every layer. The slaw should be evenly coated but not swimming in liquid.

4.  **Finish:** Scatter the chopped cilantro, peanuts, and toasted sesame seeds over the top. Toss once more gently. Serve immediately for maximum crunch, or refrigerate up to 2 hours -- it actually improves slightly as the flavors meld.
