## Chef's Note

Quesadillas are Mexico's answer to the weeknight question: how do you feed hungry people in 20 minutes? They're assembled, not cooked - just cheese, protein, and heat between two tortillas. The key is using proper melting cheese, not pre-shredded (which has anti-caking agents that prevent proper melting). Pico de gallo takes 5 minutes and transforms this from a quick dinner into something fresh and bright.

## Directions

1. **Make the Pico:** In a small bowl, combine diced tomatoes, minced onion, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, and salt. Taste and adjust. Set aside. This stays fresh for up to 3 hours.

2. **Warm Tortillas:** Heat a skillet over medium-low heat for 1 minute. Wrap tortillas in a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm as you work. This takes 3-4 minutes to get them pliable.

3. **Assemble:** Lay one tortilla flat on a plate. Sprinkle half with shredded chicken, diced jalapeño, diced onion, and cheese. Fold the tortilla in half. Repeat with remaining tortillas - you should have 4 quesadillas.

4. **Cook (Method 1: Skillet):** Heat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 quesadillas and cook for 2-3 minutes on the first side until golden and crispy. Flip and cook another 1-2 minutes until the cheese is melted and the second side is golden. Transfer to a plate. Repeat with remaining quesadillas.

5. **Cook (Method 2: Griddle - Faster):** If you have a flat griddle, heat it over medium-high heat with 2 tbsp oil. Cook all 4 quesadillas at once - 2-3 minutes per side.

6. **Serve:** Cut quesadillas into triangles. Serve immediately with pico de gallo, crema, and lime wedges on the side.

**For Kids:** Make some quesadillas with just cheese and chicken, no jalapeños, and serve pico on the side for adults. No judgment - it still tastes great.
