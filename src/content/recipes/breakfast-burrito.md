## Chef's Note

A good breakfast burrito is all about assembly order and restraint -- overfill it and it becomes an unwrappable mess. Cooking the peppers and onions first gives them time to soften and release their moisture before the eggs go in, preventing a soggy tortilla. Warming the tortillas in a dry skillet for 15-20 seconds per side makes them pliable enough to fold tightly without cracking. These freeze beautifully: wrap tightly in foil, freeze for up to a month, and reheat in a 375 degree F oven for 20 minutes straight from frozen.

## Directions

1.  **Cook Vegetables:** Melt butter in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add bell pepper and onion. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
2.  **Scramble Eggs:** Whisk eggs with milk and salt. Pour into the skillet with the vegetables. Stir gently with a spatula, folding from the edges to the center, until the eggs are just set but still slightly glossy, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat immediately.
3.  **Warm Tortillas:** In a separate dry skillet over medium-high heat, warm each tortilla for 15-20 seconds per side until pliable and lightly toasted.
4.  **Assemble:** Lay each tortilla flat. Layer in the center: a quarter of the scrambled egg mixture, a quarter of the cheese, and a quarter of the cooked bacon or sausage. Add optional toppings if desired.
5.  **Roll:** Fold the bottom edge of the tortilla up over the filling, fold both sides in, then roll tightly away from you to seal. The cheese should melt from the residual heat of the eggs.
6.  **Serve:** Serve immediately with salsa and hot sauce on the side. For meal prep, wrap each burrito tightly in foil before the cheese is added, let cool, and freeze.
