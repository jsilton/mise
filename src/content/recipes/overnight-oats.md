## Chef's Note

Overnight oats are essentially Bircher muesli, invented by Swiss physician Maximilian Bircher-Benner around 1900. The principle is simple: oats hydrate overnight in liquid, softening into a creamy, pudding-like texture without any heat. Greek yogurt adds protein and tanginess, while chia seeds absorb liquid and create body. The ratio that consistently works is 1:1 oats to liquid plus a quarter-cup of yogurt. Old-fashioned rolled oats are essential -- instant oats turn to mush and steel-cut oats stay crunchy. This is a base recipe; the variations are endless.

## Directions

1.  **Combine:** In a jar or airtight container, stir together oats, milk, yogurt, chia seeds, maple syrup, vanilla, and salt. Make sure the chia seeds are distributed throughout -- they clump if left on the surface.
2.  **Refrigerate:** Seal the container and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight (up to 5 days).
3.  **Adjust:** In the morning, stir the oats. If they are too thick, add a splash of milk and stir to loosen. If too thin, add a spoonful of oats and let sit for 10 minutes.
4.  **Top and Serve:** Add your toppings of choice. Serve cold straight from the jar, or microwave for 1-2 minutes if you prefer warm oats.

### Variation Ideas

- **PB&J:** Stir in 1 tbsp peanut butter and top with smashed raspberries.
- **Banana Nut:** Add half a mashed banana to the base mixture and top with walnuts and a drizzle of honey.
- **Berry:** Top with mixed berries and a sprinkle of granola.
- **Carrot Cake:** Stir in 1/4 cup shredded carrot, a handful of raisins, and a pinch of cinnamon. Top with walnuts and a drizzle of maple syrup.
- **Tropical:** Use coconut milk and top with mango, toasted coconut, and macadamia nuts.
