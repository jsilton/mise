## Chef's Note

This salad bridges lunch and dinner  -  hearty enough with farro's chewy texture to anchor a meal, but light enough for summer eating. Farro is the grain of ancient Rome and remains central to Mediterranean cooking because it has more fiber and protein than wheat, and a nutty flavor that holds up to bold olive oil and vinegar. Make it ahead without the herbs and feta; they stay bright when added just before serving or at the table.

## Directions

1. **Cook the Farro:** Rinse the farro under cold water. In a medium pot, bring broth and salt to a boil. Add farro, stir once, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 25-30 minutes until tender but still chewy (not mushy). Drain any excess liquid and spread on a sheet pan to cool to room temperature  -  this keeps the grains from clumping.

2. **Prepare the Vegetables:** While the farro cools, dice the cucumber, tomatoes, and bell pepper into roughly 1/2-inch pieces. Finely dice the red onion. Place all in a large bowl with the olives.

3. **Make the Dressing:** In a small jar or bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, grated garlic, and black pepper. Taste and adjust acidity  -  it should be bright and tangy.

4. **Combine:** Add the cooled farro to the bowl with the vegetables. Pour the dressing over and toss well to coat every grain and vegetable.

5. **Finish:** Just before serving, fold in the crumbled feta, parsley, mint, and dill. Taste and adjust salt and lemon juice. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

6. **Make-Ahead:** This salad tastes even better the next day as flavors meld. Store covered in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Add the feta and fresh herbs just before serving to prevent them from getting soggy.
