## Chef's Note

Stuffed shells (conchiglioni ripieni) are Italian-American "pasta al forno" that became a staple of red-sauce joints in the 1950s-70s. The three things that make or break this dish: squeeze the spinach bone-dry (any residual water makes the filling weep), use enough ricotta to actually fill the shells (15 oz minimum for a 12 oz box  -  10 oz runs out halfway through), and never use a food processor for the filling  -  it purées the ricotta into a runny paste. Hand-mix with a spatula so the filling stays thick and fluffy. These assemble ahead beautifully  -  build the pan, refrigerate overnight, bake when ready.

## Directions

1. **The Spinach (Sauté, Don't Blanch):** Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the smashed garlic clove to perfume the oil for 1 minute, then add spinach. Sauté 2-3 minutes until just wilted. Discard the garlic. Let cool, then wrap in a clean kitchen towel and wring out until **bone dry**  -  this is non-negotiable. Chop finely with a knife.

2. **The Shells:** Boil shells in well-salted water for **2-3 minutes less** than package directions  -  they should be firm and pliable, not soft. They finish cooking in the oven. Drain, spread on a sheet pan, and drizzle with olive oil so they don't stick.

3. **The Three-Cheese Filling:** In a large bowl, combine ricotta, 1 cup shredded mozzarella, 1/3 cup Parmigiano, beaten egg, dry chopped spinach, lemon zest, nutmeg, chives, 1/2 tsp salt, and pepper. **Fold gently with a spatula**  -  stop the moment everything is combined. The filling should be thick and scoopable. (Chef's trick: scoop into a gallon Ziploc, snip one corner, and pipe directly into shells.)

4. **The Architecture:** Preheat oven to 375°F. Spread 1 cup of sauce across the bottom of a 9x13 baking dish  -  this prevents sticking and steaming.

5. **Fill and Arrange:** Hold a cooled shell in your palm, pipe or spoon a generous amount of filling inside, and nestle it open-side up in the dish. Repeat until the pan is snug. Spoon remaining sauce over the tops  -  **make sure sauce covers the pasta edges** so they don't dry out and get crunchy. Sprinkle remaining mozzarella and Parmigiano over everything.

6. **The Bake:** Cover tightly with foil (don't let it touch the cheese). Bake 25 minutes covered. Remove foil, bake another 15-20 minutes until edges are bubbling and cheese is golden with browned spots.

7. **Rest:** Let the pan sit 10 minutes before serving  -  the cheese sets up and the filling stays inside the shells when you plate.
