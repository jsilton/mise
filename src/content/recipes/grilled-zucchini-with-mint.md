## Chef's Note

Zucchini is best when treated aggressively -- high heat, minimal time, strong seasoning. Slicing lengthwise into planks gives you maximum surface area for charring and prevents the pieces from falling through the grill grates. The mint and lemon go on after cooking because heat destroys their brightness. This is a summer side that comes together in minutes and pairs with virtually anything off the grill.

## Directions

1.  **Heat the Grill:** Preheat grill or grill pan to high heat (450-500F). Clean and oil the grates.

2.  **Prep:** Slice zucchini lengthwise into 1/4-inch thick planks. Brush both sides with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

3.  **Grill:** Place zucchini planks directly on the grill grates perpendicular to the bars. Grill for 2-3 minutes per side until distinct grill marks appear and the zucchini is tender but not floppy. You want some resistance when you press the center.

4.  **Dress:** Transfer grilled zucchini to a serving platter. Immediately drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and lemon juice. Scatter torn mint leaves and lemon zest over the top. Finish with a pinch of flaky sea salt.

5.  **Serve:** Serve warm or at room temperature. This dish actually improves after sitting for 10-15 minutes as the dressing soaks in.
