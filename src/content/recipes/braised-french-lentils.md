## Chef's Note

French green lentils (lentilles du Puy) are the only lentils worth braising as a side dish. Unlike their brown or red cousins, they hold their shape beautifully and develop a firm, almost al dente bite. Building the flavor base with a proper mirepoix -- onion, carrot, celery sauteed until softened -- gives these humble legumes a backbone of savory depth. The splash of vinegar and mustard at the end is essential: it lifts the earthiness and prevents the dish from tasting flat.

## Directions

1.  **Build the Base:** Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven or heavy saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 6-7 minutes until the vegetables are softened and the onion is translucent. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more until fragrant.

2.  **Add Lentils and Aromatics:** Stir in the lentils, thyme sprigs, and bay leaf. Pour in the broth and stir to combine. Bring to a boil over high heat.

3.  **Braise:** Reduce heat to low, partially cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are tender but still hold their shape. They should be creamy inside with a slight bite. Most of the liquid should be absorbed -- if it evaporates too quickly, add a splash of water.

4.  **Finish:** Remove the thyme sprigs and bay leaf. Stir in the Dijon mustard and red wine vinegar. Season with salt and pepper. Fold in the fresh parsley just before serving.
