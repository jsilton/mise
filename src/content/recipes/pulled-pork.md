## Chef's Note

Pulled pork is a patience recipe — the slow cooker does all the work over 10-11 hours while you go about your day. The rub builds a deep layer of smoky sweetness on the surface while the acidic braising liquid (vinegar and Worcestershire) breaks down the collagen into silky gelatin. Bone-in shoulder is essential — the bone conducts heat to the center and the surrounding connective tissue renders into the richest juices. No sauce needed. Just pull out the bone and fat, chop the meat, and let it sit in its own cooking liquid.

## Directions

1. **The Rub:** Mix brown sugar, smoked salt, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, and black pepper in a small bowl. Massage the rub into all surfaces of the pork shoulder, getting into every crevice. If you have time, season the night before and refrigerate uncovered for even deeper flavor.

2. **The Pot Base:** Scatter the chopped onion and smashed garlic across the bottom of a 6-quart slow cooker. Pour in the apple cider vinegar and Worcestershire sauce.

3. **The Load:** Place the rubbed pork shoulder on top of the aromatics, fat cap up. Do not add any extra liquid — the pork will release plenty of its own juices as it cooks.

4. **The Cook:** Cover and cook on LOW for 10-11 hours. The pork is done when it pulls apart effortlessly with a fork and the bone slides out clean. Do not rush it — anything under 9 hours and the collagen has not fully broken down.

5. **The Finish:** Transfer the pork to a cutting board. Pull out the bone — it should slide right out. Remove any large pieces of fat or gristle. Chop the meat into rough pieces.

6. **The Juice:** Skim excess fat from the cooking liquid in the slow cooker. Return the chopped pork to the pot and toss in just enough of the cooking liquid to keep it moist and flavorful.

7. **Serve:** Pile on plates alongside cornbread and coleslaw, or use as the base for tacos, bowls, and sandwiches all week.
