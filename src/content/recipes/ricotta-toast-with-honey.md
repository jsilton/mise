## Chef's Note

Whipping ricotta with olive oil and a pinch of salt transforms grainy supermarket ricotta into something cloud-like and spreadable. The technique takes 30 seconds with a fork and the result is dramatically better than spreading ricotta straight from the container. Whole-milk ricotta is essential -- part-skim versions are watery and lack the richness needed to stand up to the honey and pistachios. The contrast of sweet honey, salty pistachios, and creamy ricotta on crunchy toast creates a breakfast that feels indulgent but comes together faster than scrambled eggs.

## Directions

1.  **Whip the Ricotta:** In a small bowl, combine ricotta, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Stir vigorously with a fork for 30 seconds until the mixture is smooth, fluffy, and spreadable. It should look noticeably lighter and creamier than it did straight from the container.
2.  **Toast:** Toast the bread until deeply golden and crunchy. The bread must be sturdy enough to support the ricotta without bending.
3.  **Spread:** Divide the whipped ricotta between the two toasts, spreading it in a thick, generous layer to the edges.
4.  **Top:** Drizzle honey in a zigzag pattern across the ricotta. Scatter chopped pistachios over the top. Add a pinch of flaky sea salt.
5.  **Finish:** Add optional toppings -- a few torn mint leaves, a fine grating of lemon zest, or halved fresh figs if in season.
6.  **Serve:** Eat immediately while the toast is still warm and crunchy. The honey will slowly seep into the ricotta, creating sweet pockets throughout.
