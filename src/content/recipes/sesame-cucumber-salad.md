## Chef's Note

Smashing cucumbers instead of slicing them is the defining technique of this Sichuan classic. A flat whack with a knife or rolling pin cracks the flesh into jagged, irregular pieces that absorb the dressing into every crevice -- something smooth slices cannot do. The mandatory salting step pulls out excess water so the dressing clings rather than pooling at the bottom of the bowl. Chinkiang (black) vinegar gives the authentic smoky-sweet depth, but rice vinegar is a reasonable substitute.

## Directions

1.  **Smash:** Place cucumbers on a cutting board. Lay the flat side of a large knife or cleaver on top and press down firmly with the heel of your hand until the cucumber cracks open. Cut into rough 1-inch pieces.
2.  **Salt:** Toss the smashed cucumbers with 1 tsp salt in a colander set over a bowl. Let sit for 10-15 minutes to draw out excess water. Press gently to remove more liquid -- do not rinse.
3.  **Make the Dressing:** In a large bowl, whisk together garlic, soy sauce, black vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and chili crisp.
4.  **Combine:** Add the drained cucumbers to the dressing and toss until every piece is coated.
5.  **Finish:** Sprinkle with sesame seeds and scallions. Serve immediately -- this salad becomes watery if it sits too long.
