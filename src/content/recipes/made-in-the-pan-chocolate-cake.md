## Chef's Note

This cake is pure American efficiency—everything mixes right in the pan, no bowls to clean. The trick is understanding what each ingredient does: vinegar reacts with baking soda to create lift and crumb structure, cocoa powder needs cold water to bloom properly, and the whole formula relies on mixing order so ingredients emulsify correctly into a smooth, lump-free batter.
## Directions

1.  **The Dry Mix:** Preheat oven to 325°F. In an 8x8-inch square baking dish, whisk the flour, sugar, cocoa, baking soda, and salt until uniform. Use your fingers to crush any cocoa lumps.
2.  **The Wells:** Make three wells in the dry mix. Pour the oil into one, the vanilla into the second, and the vinegar into the third.
3.  **Combine:** Pour the cold water over everything. Using a fork or small whisk, stir in circular motions until a smooth batter forms. Scrape the corners of the pan carefully.
4.  **Bake:** Sprinkle the chocolate chips over the top. Wipe the edges of the dish with a damp towel for a clean "Kitchen Standard" look.
5.  **Cook:** Bake for 28-33 minutes until a toothpick comes out with just a few moist crumbs.
6.  **Finish:** Dust with confectioners' sugar once cool for the mandatory **High Note** visual.
