## Chef's Note

The secret to spring rolls that hold together is the rice paper hydration -- too wet and it tears, too dry and it cracks. Dip the wrapper in warm (not hot) water for just 2-3 seconds; it will continue to soften as you fill it. Roll tightly like a burrito, tucking the sides in after the first fold. These are best eaten within an hour of assembly, so prep all components ahead and roll just before serving.

## Directions

1.  **Cook the Shrimp:** Bring a medium saucepan of salted water to a boil. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until pink and curled. Transfer to an ice bath immediately. Once cool, slice each shrimp in half lengthwise through the back. Pat dry.

2.  **Cook the Noodles:** Bring water back to a boil. Cook the rice vermicelli according to package directions (usually 3-4 minutes). Drain, rinse under cold water until completely cool, and snip into manageable lengths with scissors.

3.  **Prep the Vegetables:** Arrange the lettuce, herbs, julienned carrot, cucumber, cooked noodles, and halved shrimp on a large cutting board or platter for easy assembly.

4.  **Soften the Wrappers:** Fill a wide, shallow dish with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper in for 2-3 seconds -- it should still feel slightly firm. Lay it flat on a clean, damp work surface.

5.  **Fill and Roll:** On the lower third of the wrapper, layer a small handful of lettuce, a pinch of noodles, a few pieces of carrot and cucumber, and some herbs. Fold the bottom edge up and over the filling, tuck in the sides firmly, then place 3-4 shrimp halves cut-side down along the fold line. Continue rolling tightly until sealed. The shrimp will show through the translucent wrapper.

6.  **Make the Dipping Sauce:** Whisk together the hoisin sauce, peanut butter, sriracha, and warm water until smooth. Adjust sriracha to taste. Garnish with crushed peanuts.

7.  **Serve:** Cut each roll in half on the diagonal with a sharp, wet knife. Serve immediately with the peanut hoisin dipping sauce.
