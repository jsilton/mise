## Chef's Note

Quinoa has a reputation for being bland and virtuous, but that is a failure of technique, not the grain itself. Rinsing is non-negotiable -- the natural saponin coating tastes bitter and soapy if you skip it. Toasting the rinsed quinoa briefly before adding liquid deepens the flavor considerably. The herbs and lemon go in after cooking and off-heat so they stay vibrant. This base works under grilled proteins, roasted vegetables, or scooped into bowls with any Mediterranean or Middle Eastern main.

## Directions

1.  **Rinse:** Place quinoa in a fine-mesh strainer and rinse under cold running water for 30 seconds, rubbing the grains between your fingers. This removes the bitter saponin coating.

2.  **Toast (Optional):** Heat a dry saucepan over medium heat. Add the rinsed, drained quinoa and stir for 2 minutes until the water evaporates and the grains start to smell nutty and pop slightly.

3.  **Cook:** Add water or broth and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest simmer, cover tightly, and cook for 15 minutes. Do not lift the lid during cooking.

4.  **Rest:** Remove from heat and let sit, covered, for 5 minutes. The residual steam finishes cooking the quinoa and ensures fluffy individual grains.

5.  **Dress:** Fluff the quinoa with a fork. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, then fold in lemon zest, parsley, mint, dill, and pepper. Taste and adjust salt. Serve warm, at room temperature, or chilled.
