## Chef's Note

Roasting cauliflower whole transforms it from a humble brassica into something dramatic and deeply flavored. The exterior develops a caramelized crust while the interior turns creamy and almost custard-like. Tahini drizzle adds richness and nuttiness without heaviness. The key is a very hot oven and patience -- do not pull it early. The deep golden color is where all the flavor lives.

## Directions

1.  **Preheat:** Set oven to 425F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.

2.  **Prep the Cauliflower:** Remove outer leaves and trim the stem so the cauliflower sits flat. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Rub the spiced oil all over the cauliflower, working it into the crevices.

3.  **Roast:** Place cauliflower cut-side down on the prepared sheet pan. Roast for 35-40 minutes, until deeply golden on the outside and a knife slides easily into the center with no resistance.

4.  **Make the Tahini Sauce:** While the cauliflower roasts, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, warm water, and minced garlic in a small bowl until smooth and drizzleable. Add more water a teaspoon at a time if too thick.

5.  **Finish:** Transfer the roasted cauliflower to a serving platter. Drizzle generously with tahini sauce. Scatter with parsley, pomegranate seeds, and Aleppo pepper if using. Serve whole and let diners break off pieces at the table.
