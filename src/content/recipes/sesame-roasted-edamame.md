## Chef's Note

Japanese snack culture celebrates simple roasted vegetables, and edamame becomes addictive when roasted at high heat until crispy. The sesame oil and seeds provide nuttiness that transforms plain edamame into something kids won't stop eating. Roasting at 400°F drives off moisture and creates crispy, salty exterior while keeping the bean tender inside. These hold well for leftovers - serve at room temperature as a snack.

## Directions

1. **Thaw:** If using frozen edamame, thaw them and pat completely dry with paper towels. Moisture is the enemy of crispiness.
2. **Preheat:** Set oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
3. **Toss:** In a bowl, combine the dried edamame with sesame oil and soy sauce. Toss well to coat evenly. Spread in a single layer on the sheet - don't crowd.
4. **Roast:** Bake for 12-15 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through, until the edamame are golden and crispy. Watch during the last few minutes to avoid burning.
5. **Season:** Remove from oven. While still hot, toss with sesame seeds, salt, garlic powder, and red pepper flakes if using. Taste and adjust salt.
6. **Serve:** Serve warm or at room temperature as a side or snack.
