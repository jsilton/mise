## Chef's Note

Fried rice is Cantonese resourcefulness - turning yesterday's rice into tonight's dinner. The absolute requirement is day-old rice, which dries out slightly and fries instead of steaming into a gummy clump. Cold rice with grains that separate is the entire foundation of the dish. If you only have fresh rice, spread it on a plate and chill it for 30 minutes. This is not a trick; this is the technique.

## Directions

1. **Rice Prep:** Day-old rice is essential. If using freshly cooked rice, spread it on a plate and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Break the cold rice into individual grains with your fingers before cooking.

2. **Mise en Place:** Have beaten eggs, garlic, green onions, vegetables, and sauce (soy and sesame oil mixed together) ready before the pan heats.

3. **Heat the Wok:** Place a wok or large skillet over high heat for 2 minutes until smoking. Add 1 tbsp vegetable oil and let it smoke for 15 seconds.

4. **Cook the Eggs:** Pour in beaten eggs and scramble quickly, breaking them into small pieces. Cook for 1 minute until just set but still slightly moist. Transfer to a plate.

5. **Cook the Rice:** Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add cold rice and break apart any clumps with a wooden spoon. Stir constantly for 3-4 minutes until the rice is heated through and grains are separated. You should hear the rice hitting the sides of the wok - that's the sound of success.

6. **Aromatics and Vegetables:** Push rice to the side. Add garlic and stir for 15 seconds. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 1 minute.

7. **Combine:** Return eggs to the wok. Add soy sauce and sesame oil. Toss everything together for 30 seconds until coated evenly. Add white pepper.

8. **Finish:** Transfer to a serving bowl. Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds.

**The Cold Rice Rule:** This is not optional. Fresh rice = gummy fried rice. Day-old rice = individual, fluffy grains.
