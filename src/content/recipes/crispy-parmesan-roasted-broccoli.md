## Chef's Note

High heat is the entire strategy here: at 425°F, broccoli florets crisp on the outside while staying tender inside, and the cheese creates a salty, nutty crust that kids will ask for seconds on. The key is spreading the broccoli in a single layer without crowding - steamed broccoli is the enemy. Pat the florets dry before roasting so the cheese adheres and crisps rather than sliding off.

## Directions

1. **Prep:** Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper. Pat broccoli florets completely dry with a clean kitchen towel.
2. **Season:** Toss broccoli with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer on the sheet - do not crowd or they will steam rather than roast.
3. **Roast:** Bake for 15 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through.
4. **Add Cheese:** Remove from oven. Sprinkle the entire batch evenly with grated Parmigiano-Reggiano. Return to the oven.
5. **Crisp:** Bake for 5 more minutes until the cheese is melted and the edges are golden brown and crispy. The cheese should have some dark spots.
6. **Finish:** Sprinkle with flaky sea salt and a pinch of red pepper flakes if desired. Serve immediately while crispy.
