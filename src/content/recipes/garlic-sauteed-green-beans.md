## Chef's Note

The most common mistake with sauteed green beans is overcrowding the pan, which creates steam instead of sear. A screaming hot skillet and a single layer of beans gives you blistered, bright green results in under 10 minutes. Adding the garlic after the beans have charred prevents it from burning. A squeeze of lemon at the end is not optional -- it lifts the entire dish and keeps the green beans tasting vibrant rather than flat.

## Directions

1.  **Dry:** Make sure the trimmed green beans are completely dry. Wet beans will steam instead of blister.
2.  **Sear:** Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat until it just begins to smoke. Add green beans in a single layer -- work in batches if needed. Let them sit undisturbed for 2 minutes until blistered and charred in spots.
3.  **Toss:** Toss the beans and cook for another 2-3 minutes, shaking the pan occasionally, until tender-crisp and bright green with dark spots throughout.
4.  **Aromatics:** Push beans to one side. Add garlic and red pepper flakes to the open space and cook for 30-45 seconds until the garlic is fragrant and pale gold. Toss everything together.
5.  **Season:** Remove from heat. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice. Toss once more and serve immediately.
