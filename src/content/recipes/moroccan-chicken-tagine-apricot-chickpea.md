## Chef's Note

A classic Moroccan tagine with apricots and almonds draws from the sweet-savory tradition of the Maghreb — specifically Fez, where medieval trade routes brought dried fruit, saffron, and almonds into everyday cooking. Preserved lemon replaces olives here, which is a fair trade: it carries the same briny, floral note that cuts through the sweetness, without the olives themselves. The one thing worth insisting on is bone-in, skin-on chicken — the skin crisps in the initial sear, the bones enrich the braising liquid, and the whole pot tastes flatter without them.

## Directions

1. **Mix the spice blend:** Combine all spices, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Pat chicken thighs dry and rub **half** the spice blend all over the chicken. Let sit 15 minutes (or up to overnight) while you prep the rest.
2. **Sear the chicken:** Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a dutch oven over medium-high. Sear chicken skin-side down until deep golden brown — **6-8 minutes, don't move them**. Flip and sear 2 minutes more. Transfer to a plate. _(Don't worry if they're not cooked through — they finish in the braise.)_
3. **Build the base:** Pour off all but 1 tbsp fat. Add remaining 1 tbsp olive oil. Add onions with a pinch of salt and cook until deeply soft and beginning to caramelize — **10-12 minutes**. Add garlic and remaining spice blend, stir for 60 seconds until fragrant. Add tomato paste and cook 1 minute to darken.
4. **Deglaze and braise:** Pour in chicken stock, scraping up the fond. Add chickpeas, apricots, preserved lemon, bloomed saffron (with its water), cinnamon stick, and honey. Stir to combine. Nestle chicken thighs back in skin-side up — they should be half-submerged, skin staying above the liquid to stay crisp.
5. **Slow simmer:** Bring to a gentle simmer, then cover and reduce to low. Cook **45-50 minutes** until chicken is falling-off-the-bone tender and the sauce has thickened. For the last 10 minutes, uncover to re-crisp the chicken skin and reduce the sauce slightly.
6. **Toast the almonds:** While the tagine finishes, toast slivered almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat, stirring constantly, until golden — **3-4 minutes**. Watch closely, they burn in seconds.
7. **Finish and rest:** Remove from heat. Stir in lemon juice and half the cilantro and parsley. Taste — it should be sweet-warm-savory with a briny pop from the preserved lemon. Adjust salt. Let rest 10 minutes for flavors to settle.
8. **Serve:** Scatter toasted almonds and remaining herbs over the top. Serve over pearl couscous with extra sauce spooned around.
9. **Reheat (day 2 or 3):** Tagine improves substantially overnight — the apricots plump further, the spices marry, the chicken absorbs more sauce. Reheat covered at 325°F for 20-25 minutes with a splash of water or stock. Add fresh herbs and almonds at the table; never reheat the toasted almonds (they go soft).
