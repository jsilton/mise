## Chef's Note

Great chana masala gets its depth from blooming whole cumin seeds in hot oil before anything else hits the pan -- this 30-second step unlocks flavor that ground cumin alone cannot deliver. The other key is using amchur (dried mango powder) for the signature tangy punch. If you cannot find it, a squeeze of lemon juice at the end works. Resist the urge to add too much water; the curry should be thick and coating, not soupy.

## Directions

1.  **Bloom the Spices:** Heat the oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the cumin seeds and cook for 30 seconds until they sizzle, darken, and become fragrant. Do not let them burn.

2.  **Cook the Aromatics:** Add the diced onion and cook for 6-8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply golden. Add the garlic, ginger, and slit green chiles. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

3.  **Build the Masala:** Add the ground coriander, 1 tsp of the garam masala, turmeric, amchur, and red chile powder. Stir for 30 seconds to toast the spices in the oil, then immediately add the crushed tomatoes. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently, until the oil begins to separate from the tomato mixture.

4.  **Add the Chickpeas:** Stir in the drained chickpeas, water, and salt. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Using the back of a wooden spoon, smash about a quarter of the chickpeas against the side of the pan to thicken the sauce.

5.  **Finish:** Stir in the remaining 1 tsp garam masala and the lemon juice. Taste and adjust salt and acidity. The curry should be thick, saucy, and tangy.

6.  **Serve:** Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with basmati rice or warm naan.
