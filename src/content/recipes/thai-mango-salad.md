## Chef's Note

This salad is a masterclass in Thai flavor balance: spicy from the chilies, sour from the lime juice, salty from the fish sauce, and the subtle sweetness from unripe mango and honey. Use green (unripe) mangoes, not ripe ones  -  the unripe fruit has crisp texture and tart flavor that make this salad sing. If you can't find green mangoes, young papaya works as a substitute. Fish sauce is non-negotiable  -  it provides umami depth that transforms this from a simple fruit salad into something complex and crave-worthy.

## Directions

1. **Prepare the Mango:** Peel the green mangoes using a vegetable peeler or sharp knife. Julienne the flesh into thin matchsticks. Set aside in a large mixing bowl.

2. **Make the Dressing:** In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the lime juice, fish sauce, honey, and grated garlic. Taste  -  it should be balanced between bright (lime), salty (fish sauce), and subtly sweet (honey). Adjust to your preference.

3. **Combine:** Pour the dressing over the mango and toss to coat. Add the chopped peanuts, Thai basil, mint, cilantro, green onions, and sliced chilies. Toss gently but thoroughly to distribute all the flavors evenly.

4. **Serve:** Serve immediately at room temperature. This salad is best eaten fresh  -  the herbs will begin to darken after 30 minutes, and the mango will continue to soften.

5. **Note:** This salad does not keep well as leftovers. Make it fresh to order if possible. If you do have leftover components, store the dressing separately and assemble just before serving.
