## Chef's Note

British cookbook author Meera Sodha learned this naan from her aunt Harsha and published it in her 2015 book "Made in India." Instead of a tandoor, it cooks in a hot, dry sauté pan on the stovetop — no special equipment — with yogurt lending a faint tang and a soft, yielding crumb. Set up an assembly line to roll and cook, and after a couple of tries you'll never buy naan again.

## Directions

1. **Make the Dough:** Put the flour into a large mixing bowl and make a well in the middle. Add 2 tablespoons of the oil along with the yogurt, yeast, sugar, salt, and baking powder. Use your fingers to combine until the mixture resembles crumbs, then add the warm milk in splashes, mixing until it comes together into a dough.

2. **Knead:** Turn the dough out onto a clean, well-floured board — it will be very sticky. Flour your hands and knead for about 5 minutes, scraping the dough off your hands as needed, until it forms a smooth round ball. Rub 1 teaspoon of oil over the exterior.

3. **Rise:** Place the dough in a clean bowl, cover with a dish towel, and set in a warm spot to rise for 60 to 90 minutes, until doubled in size.

4. **Divide and Roll:** Using a knife, divide the dough into 12 pieces. Roll each into a ball and flatten between your palms. Dust with flour and roll each piece into an oval about 5 by 8 inches. (Roll them all out first, assembly-line style, so you can cook without stopping.)

5. **Cook:** Set a large sauté pan over medium-high heat until hot. Cook one naan for 30 to 40 seconds, until it begins to bubble, then flip and cook the other side about the same, watching closely so it doesn't burn. Flip once more and press lightly with a spatula for 10 to 15 seconds, then move to a warm platter. Repeat with the rest, keeping the finished breads in a low oven or wrapped in foil until ready to serve. _For more tandoor-like leopard-spotting, use a cast-iron skillet and run it a shade hotter — the heavier, hotter surface blisters the bubbles faster._

6. **Optional Garlic-Butter Brush (for garlic naan):** Warm the melted butter with the minced garlic until just fragrant, about 30 seconds — don't let it brown. Brush over each hot naan as it comes off the pan. This is the version you want for mopping up curry.
