## Chef's Note

The soul of great pho bo lives in the charred aromatics and the long, patient simmer. Blackening the onions and ginger under the broiler builds a smoky sweetness that no shortcut can replicate. The most common mistake is boiling the broth too hard, which turns it cloudy and greasy. Keep it at a bare simmer -- you want lazy bubbles, not a rolling boil. Parboiling the bones first and rinsing them clean is the single most important step for a clear, golden broth.

## Directions

1.  **Parboil the Bones:** Place the marrow bones and brisket in a large stockpot. Cover with cold water and bring to a vigorous boil for 10 minutes. Drain, rinse the bones and meat under cold water, and scrub the pot clean. This removes impurities and is essential for a clear broth.

2.  **Char the Aromatics:** While the bones parboil, place the halved onions and ginger cut-side down on a sheet pan. Broil on high for 12-15 minutes until deeply blackened and softened. The char is critical for flavor.

3.  **Toast the Spices:** In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the star anise, cinnamon stick, cloves, coriander seeds, and cardamom pod for 2-3 minutes until fragrant. Place them in a spice sachet or cheesecloth bundle.

4.  **Build the Broth:** Return the cleaned bones and brisket to the scrubbed pot. Add 6 quarts cold water, charred aromatics, and spice bundle. Bring to a boil, then immediately reduce to the gentlest possible simmer. Skim any foam that rises during the first 30 minutes.

5.  **Remove the Brisket:** After 1.5-2 hours, check the brisket with a chopstick. When it slides through with little resistance, transfer the brisket to an ice bath for 10 minutes (this firms the meat for slicing), then refrigerate. Continue simmering the bones for 2 more hours.

6.  **Season the Broth:** After a total of 4 hours, strain the broth through a fine-mesh strainer lined with cheesecloth. Discard solids. Stir in the fish sauce, sugar, and salt. Taste and adjust -- the broth should be deeply savory with a hint of sweetness.

7.  **Prepare the Noodles:** Soak the rice noodles in warm water for 20-25 minutes until pliable. Just before serving, cook in rapidly boiling water for 30-60 seconds until tender. Drain immediately.

8.  **Slice the Meats:** Slice the chilled brisket across the grain into thin pieces. Arrange the raw eye of round slices separately -- the hot broth will cook them in the bowl.

9.  **Assemble:** Divide noodles among deep bowls. Top with sliced brisket and raw eye of round. Ladle the boiling-hot broth over the meat (the raw beef will turn pink and cook instantly). Serve immediately with a platter of bean sprouts, Thai basil, cilantro, lime wedges, jalapenos, hoisin, and sriracha.
