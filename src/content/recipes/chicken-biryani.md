## Chef's Note

Hyderabadi biryani is the dish Nizam-era royal kitchens refined into what many Indians consider the country's most technically demanding rice preparation — specifically the _kacchi_ method, where raw marinated chicken and par-cooked rice are layered and sealed, cooking together in the pot. The whole architecture rests on two things you cannot rush: marinate the chicken for at least 4 hours (the yogurt tenderizes and carries spice deep into the meat), and fry the onions until they are mahogany-dark and crisp — pale onions make pale biryani. Wedding cooks will tell you the test is the smell: if the steam coming out of the pot at the end doesn't stop you in your tracks, you under-seasoned somewhere.

## Directions

1. **Marinate the chicken (night before):** Whisk yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, all spices, salt, lemon juice, cilantro, and mint in a bowl. Add chicken and toss to coat every piece. Cover and refrigerate **at least 4 hours, ideally overnight**. The longer the better.
2. **Fry the onions (birista):** Heat 1/2 cup oil in a wide skillet over medium-high. Add sliced onions and fry, stirring occasionally, until **deep mahogany brown and crisp — 15-20 minutes**. Do not stop early; pale onions are the #1 mistake. Drain on paper towels. Reserve 3 tbsp of the onion oil for layering.
3. **Bloom saffron:** Warm the milk (don't boil). Add saffron threads and let steep 15 minutes — it will turn brilliant orange.
4. **Par-cook the rice:** Rinse basmati in cold water until it runs clear (4-5 changes). Bring 8 cups water to a rolling boil with 2 tbsp salt, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, bay leaves, and cumin seeds. Add rice and boil **exactly 5-6 minutes** — grains should be soft on the outside, firm in the center (pinch one to check). Drain immediately.
5. **Layer the pot:** In a heavy dutch oven, spread reserved onion oil across the bottom. Add marinated chicken in an even layer, including all the marinade. Scatter 1/3 of the fried onions over the chicken. Top with the par-cooked rice in an even layer. Drizzle saffron milk, melted ghee, rose water, and remaining fried onions over the rice. Scatter cilantro and mint on top.
6. **Seal and dum-cook:** Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid isn't snug, seal the gap with a strip of foil or a flour-water dough rope (traditional method). Cook over **medium-high for 4 minutes** to get the bottom sizzling, then reduce to the **lowest possible heat for 25-30 minutes**. A flame tamper or heat diffuser helps prevent scorching.
7. **Rest (non-negotiable):** Turn off heat and let the sealed pot sit **15 minutes** without lifting the lid. This is where the steam finishes cooking the rice top-to-bottom.
8. **Serve:** Open the lid — the smell should hit you. Gently dig down with a wide spoon from side to top, lifting rather than stirring, so each serving gets rice, chicken, and crispy onions.
9. **Reheat (day 2 or 3):** Biryani improves. Transfer portions to a baking dish, sprinkle 2 tbsp water over the rice, cover with foil, and heat at 325°F for 20-25 minutes. Fluff gently before serving.
