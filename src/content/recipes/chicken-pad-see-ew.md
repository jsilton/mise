## Chef's Note

Pad See Ew (stir-fried soy sauce noodles) is a beloved Thai street food classic that relies on high-heat stir-frying to caramelize the noodles and vegetables. The key to the dish is developing **wok hei** (breath of the wok) by leaving the noodles undisturbed in a smoking hot pan to char. Because fresh wide rice noodles (Sen Yai) are difficult to source, we use the **hot-soak method** on dried rice sticks. Soaking in hot water hydrates the noodle core so it stays chewy, preventing the noodles from breaking or turning mushy in the wok. Sweet dark soy sauce (si-ew dum) is thick and syrupy, and is non-negotiable for achieving the correct color and caramelization.

## Directions

1. **The Hot-Soak Method:** Place the dried noodles in a large bowl. Completely cover them with hot tap water (not boiling water). Let them soak for 15 to 25 minutes until they are fully pliable and opaque, but still slightly firm in the center. Drain thoroughly, then immediately toss with 1 tbsp of neutral oil to coat the noodles. This oil barrier prevents them from sticking together into a solid block.
2. **Mix the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the oyster sauce, regular soy sauce, sweet dark soy sauce, 2 tsp sugar, and 1 tbsp water until the sugar is dissolved. Set aside.
3. **Sear the Chicken & Stems:** Heat a large wok or massive skillet over high heat with 2 tbsp of neutral oil until smoking. Add the sliced chicken and the Gai Lan stems (stems take longer to cook than leaves). Sear undisturbed for 2 minutes to get a brown crust, then toss and stir-fry for 4 minutes until the chicken is cooked through. Transfer to a clean plate.
4. **Char the Noodles:** Add another 1 to 2 tbsp of oil to the smoking hot wok. Add the drained, oiled noodles in a flat layer. Let them sit **completely undisturbed for 30 to 45 seconds** to develop a charred crust.
5. **Steam-Sear:** Pour the sauce mixture over the noodles. If the noodles feel too firm (since dried noodles have less internal moisture than fresh), splash in 1 to 2 tbsp of water. This creates a quick burst of steam that cooks the noodles to chewy perfection. Toss vigorously to coat them in the caramelized sauce.
6. **Scramble the Eggs:** Push the noodles to one side of the wok. Crack the 3 eggs into the cleared space. Scramble them quickly with your spatula until just set, then toss and integrate them into the noodles.
7. **Wilt and Finish:** Return the cooked chicken, Gai Lan stems, and the raw Gai Lan leaves to the wok. Toss everything together over high heat for 60 seconds until the leaves are just wilted and bright green. Serve immediately, ideally alongside [Smashed Cucumber Salad](/mise/recipes/smashed-cucumber-salad) to cut through the richness of the noodles.
