## Chef's Note

Avocado toast is only as good as its two main ingredients: the bread and the avocado. The bread must be thick-cut and toasted until genuinely crunchy -- a soft, floppy base turns to mush under the weight of the avocado. Sourdough is ideal because its tangy flavor and sturdy crumb provide a counterpoint to the rich, buttery avocado. The avocado should be ripe (yields to gentle pressure but is not mushy) and roughly mashed with a fork rather than pureed smooth -- you want texture and variation in every bite. Acid (lemon or lime) and salt are non-negotiable; without them, avocado tastes flat.

## Directions

1.  **Toast:** Toast the bread until deeply golden and crunchy throughout -- not just colored on the surface. A proper toast should be rigid when held horizontally, not floppy.
2.  **Prep the Avocado:** Halve the avocado, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Add lemon juice and a generous pinch of flaky salt. Mash roughly with a fork, leaving some chunky pieces for texture. Do not make it smooth like guacamole.
3.  **Assemble:** Divide the avocado between the two toasts, spreading it to the edges in a thick, even layer.
4.  **Season:** Drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt and red pepper flakes.
5.  **Top (Optional):** Add any combination of toppings: a halved soft-boiled egg, sliced radishes, cherry tomato halves, pickled red onions, microgreens, or everything bagel seasoning.
6.  **Serve:** Eat immediately. Avocado toast does not wait -- it oxidizes and the bread softens within minutes.
