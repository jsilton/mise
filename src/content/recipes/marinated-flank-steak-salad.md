## Chef's Note

Flank steak is an outstanding cut for salads due to its robust beefy flavor, but its long, fibrous grain requires deliberate technique. We score the steak in a shallow diamond pattern to sever surface fibers, allowing a bright soy-dijon marinade to penetrate and tenderize the meat. Searing in a smoking-hot cast-iron skillet develops a deep caramelized crust quickly, preserving a juicy medium-rare center. Letting the meat rest is non-negotiable; cutting it too soon bleeds out the moisture, turning the lettuce soggy. Finish by slicing as thinly as possible against the grain.

## Directions

1. **Score the Steak:** Lay the flank steak flat on a cutting board. Using a sharp knife, lightly score the surface in a diagonal diamond pattern on both sides, cutting about 1/8-inch deep. This prevents the steak from curling in the pan and helps the marinade penetrate.
2. **Marinate:** In a small bowl, whisk together the marinade ingredients: 1/4 cup olive oil, soy sauce, 1 tbsp dijon mustard, minced garlic, and 1 tbsp lemon juice. Place the steak in a shallow dish or zip-top bag and pour the marinade over it. Seal and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or up to 12 hours.
3. **Bring to Temp:** Remove the steak from the refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking to take the chill off. This ensures even cooking. Pat the steak dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture so it sears rather than steams.
4. **Make the Vinaigrette:** In a jar or small bowl, combine the vinaigrette ingredients: 1/3 cup olive oil, 3 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tsp dijon mustard, minced shallot, salt, and pepper. Shake or whisk vigorously until emulsified.
5. **Sear the Steak:** Heat a cast-iron skillet over high heat until it is smoking hot. Add 1 tbsp of neutral oil (or leftover shallot oil). Lay the steak in the skillet and sear undisturbed for 4 to 5 minutes per side for medium-rare (targeting an internal temperature of 130°F).
6. **Rest:** Transfer the steak to a clean cutting board and let it rest undisturbed for 10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
7. **Slice:** Using a sharp carving knife held at a 45-degree angle, slice the rested steak as thinly as possible against the grain.
8. **Assemble:** In a large serving bowl, toss the romaine and butter lettuces with half of the vinaigrette. Arrange the sliced cucumbers, diced avocado, and shaved radishes on top. Arrange the sliced steak over the greens. Drizzle with the remaining vinaigrette.
9. **Finishing Touch:** Generously scatter the [Crispy Shallots](/mise/recipes/crispy-shallots) and toasted pepitas over the top just before serving to preserve their crunch.
