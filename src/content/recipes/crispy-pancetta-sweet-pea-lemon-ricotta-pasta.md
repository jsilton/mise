## Chef's Note

This pasta replicates the deep, emulsified richness of restaurant sauces without heavy cream by using starchy pasta water, rendered pancetta fat, and creamy ricotta. Pancetta, being cured pork belly, renders significantly more fat than prosciutto. Starting the diced pancetta in a cold skillet slowly draws out this fat and yields perfectly crispy bits. After crisping, it is important to pour off excess fat, leaving only about 1 to 2 tablespoons in the skillet to prevent the dish from becoming greasy. It is crucial to **remove the skillet from the heat** before stirring in the ricotta and Parmesan; high direct heat will cause the dairy proteins to clump and turn grainy, breaking the glossy emulsion.

## Directions

1. **Render the Pancetta:** Place the diced pancetta into a cold, large skillet. Turn the heat to medium. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 to 7 minutes until the fat renders out and the pancetta becomes crispy and golden brown. Remove the crispy pancetta with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
2. **Manage the Fat:** Carefully pour off the rendered pancetta fat from the skillet into a heat-proof dish, leaving only 1.5 to 2 tablespoons of the fat in the pan. (Save the extra pancetta fat in the fridge for roasting vegetables or frying eggs later).
3. **Build the Sauce Base:** In the same skillet containing the remaining hot pancetta fat, add the minced shallot and garlic. Cook over medium-low heat for about 2 to 3 minutes until softened and translucent, being careful not to let the garlic brown.
4. **Deglaze:** Pour in the white wine (or chicken stock). Using a wooden spoon, scrape up any browned bits stuck to the bottom of the skillet. Increase the heat slightly and let the wine bubble and reduce by half (about 2 minutes). Turn the heat down to low.
5. **Boil the Pasta:** Meanwhile, bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Season generously with kosher salt (it should taste like the sea). Drop the orecchiette and cook according to package instructions.
6. **Reserve Pasta Water:** One minute before the pasta is perfectly al dente, carefully scoop out 1 cup of the starchy cooking water and set it aside. Drain the pasta.
7. **Combine:** Add the hot pasta and sweet peas directly into the skillet with the aromatics. Toss to coat them in the reduced pan sauce.
8. **Emulsify (Off the Heat):** Remove the skillet **completely from the heat source**. Add the ricotta, grated Parmesan, lemon zest, and 1/2 cup of the reserved hot pasta water. Toss and stir vigorously for 1 to 2 minutes. The cheese and starch-filled pasta water will combine with the rendered fat to form a smooth, glossy, creamy sauce that coats the pasta. If it is too thick, add a splash more pasta water.
9. **Finish:** Season with freshly ground black pepper and taste for salt (pancetta and Parmesan are already salty, so adjust cautiously). Portion into bowls and top with the reserved crispy pancetta. Serve immediately.
