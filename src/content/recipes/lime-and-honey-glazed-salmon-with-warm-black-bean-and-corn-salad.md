## Chef's Note

Combining ingredients native to the Americas—corn, black beans, and wild salmon—this dish highlights the crossover flavors of modern Southwestern cooking. Quick, flavorful, and incredibly balanced, this meal relies on a sweet and sticky honey-lime glaze over the fish and the essential crunch of toasted pepitas to provide contrast. Serving the warm salad over a bed of fresh baby spinach keeps the greens vibrant and appealing for the whole family.

## Directions

1.  **The Prep:** Pat the salmon completely dry with paper towels and portion into 7 pieces. Season all sides with the chili powder, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp cumin, salt, and black pepper. Whisk the juice of 1 lime with the honey for the glaze; set aside.
2.  **Cook the Salmon (Main Oven or Toaster Oven):**
    - **Main Oven (Recommended):** Preheat oven to 400°F. Arrange salmon on a lightly oiled, foil-lined baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes. Brush generously with the glaze, switch to Broil on High, and broil for 2 minutes until bubbly.
    - **Toaster Oven:** Preheat broiler to High. Cook in 2 batches if necessary. Place salmon 3–4 inches from the heat source. Broil for 5–6 minutes. Brush with glaze and broil for an additional 2–3 minutes.
3.  **Char the Corn:** While the salmon cooks, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat without oil. Add the fresh corn kernels and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4–5 minutes until the corn is slightly charred and fragrant. Transfer to a bowl.
4.  **Sauté the Aromatics:** Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in the same skillet over medium heat. Stir in the red bell pepper and diced red onion. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until soft but still slightly crisp. Add garlic and sauté for 30-45 seconds.
5.  **Build the Salad:** Return the charred corn to the skillet along with the black beans, the remaining 1 tsp cumin, and red pepper flakes (if using). Cook for 2 minutes until warmed through. Remove from heat and fold in the cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.
6.  **Serve:** Create a bed of fresh baby spinach in wide bowls (this keeps the greens from getting soggy). Top with the warm corn and black bean salad, followed by a glazed salmon fillet. Garnish with diced avocado, toasted pepitas, and serve with Aromatic Purple Rice and lime wedges on the side.
