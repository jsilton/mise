## Chef's Note

Combining ingredients native to the Americas—corn, black beans, and wild salmon—this dish highlights the crossover flavors of modern Southwestern cooking. Broiling the dry-rubbed fish in a toaster oven keeps it tender, while brushing the glaze on in the final minutes creates a bubbly, caramelized crust. Quick-pickled red onions and a tangy Greek yogurt crema cut through the richness of the salmon and avocado.

## Directions

1.  **The Prep:** Pat the salmon fillets completely dry with paper towels. Season all sides with the chili powder, smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp cumin, salt, and black pepper. If any fillets have thin tail ends, tuck them under for even cooking. Arrange on a lightly oiled, foil-lined baking sheet or toaster oven tray. Whisk the juice of 1 lime with the honey for the glaze; set aside.
2.  **The Pickling & Crema:** In a small bowl, toss the thinly sliced red onion with the juice of 1 lime, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of sugar; set aside to quick-pickle. In another small bowl, whisk the Greek yogurt, the juice of 1 lime, water, and a pinch of salt until smooth and drizzling consistency.
3.  **Char the Corn:** Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat without oil. Add the fresh corn kernels and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4–5 minutes until the corn is slightly charred and fragrant. Transfer the corn to a bowl and set aside.
4.  **Broil the Salmon:** Preheat the toaster oven broiler to High. Place the salmon tray on the top rack (3–4 inches from the heat source). Broil for 5–6 minutes. Carefully slide the tray out, brush the honey-lime glaze generously over the fillets, and broil for an additional 2–3 minutes until the glaze is bubbling, glossy, and caramelized. Remove the salmon and let rest.
5.  **Sauté the Aromatics:** Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in the same skillet over medium heat. Stir in the red bell pepper and garlic. Sauté for 3 minutes until soft and fragrant.
6.  **Build the Salad:** Return the charred corn to the skillet along with the black beans, the remaining 1 tsp cumin, and red pepper flakes (if using). Pour in the chicken broth. Carefully scrape any caramelized honey and juices from the salmon foil tray directly into the skillet. Simmer for 2 minutes until the liquid reduces slightly.
7.  **The Finish:** Remove the skillet from the heat. Squeeze in the juice of the fourth lime, then fold in the baby spinach and cilantro. Toss gently for 30 seconds until the spinach just wilts in the residual heat.
8.  **Serve:** Divide the warm corn and black bean salad among wide bowls. Top each with a broiled salmon fillet, pickled red onions, diced avocado (if using), and a drizzle of the yogurt crema. Serve immediately with lime wedges and Aromatic Purple Rice on the side.
