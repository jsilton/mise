## Chef's Note

Gambas al ajillo is the ultimate proof that Spanish cooking is about restraint and great ingredients rather than complex technique. The entire dish cooks in under 5 minutes, so everything must be prepped and within arm's reach before you heat the pan. The most common mistake is overcooking the garlic -- it should be golden and fragrant, never brown or bitter. Use the best olive oil you have; it becomes the sauce. Serve it still bubbling in the pan with crusty bread to soak up every drop.

## Directions

1.  **Prep Everything First:** Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels and season with 1/2 tsp salt and the smoked paprika. Have the sliced garlic, dried peppers, sherry, parsley, and lemon juice ready beside the stove. This dish moves fast.

2.  **Heat the Oil:** Pour the olive oil into a cast-iron skillet or cazuela over medium heat. When the oil begins to shimmer, add the sliced garlic and dried guindilla peppers. Cook for 60-90 seconds, swirling constantly, until the garlic is pale golden. Do not walk away.

3.  **Add the Shrimp:** Increase heat to medium-high. Add the shrimp in a single layer and cook for 1 minute without moving them. Flip and cook for 30 seconds more.

4.  **Deglaze:** Pour in the sherry. It will bubble vigorously. Cook for 30 seconds to burn off the raw alcohol while the shrimp finish cooking through.

5.  **Finish:** Remove from heat immediately. The shrimp should be just pink and curled into a C-shape (not a tight O, which means overcooked). Squeeze the lemon juice over the pan and scatter with parsley.

6.  **Serve:** Bring the sizzling skillet directly to the table. Serve with plenty of crusty bread to mop up the garlicky, paprika-stained oil. The bread-dipping is the best part.
