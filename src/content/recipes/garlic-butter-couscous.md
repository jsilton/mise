## Chef's Note

Pearl couscous (also called Israeli couscous or ptitim) is not actually couscous at all -- it is a toasted pasta. This means it benefits from the same technique used for risotto and pilaf: toast the grains in fat first to develop a nutty flavor before adding liquid. Unlike regular couscous, pearl couscous has a satisfying chewy texture that makes it a substantial base. Cooking in broth rather than water adds depth, and the butter-garlic combination creates a simple but complete flavor foundation that pairs with nearly any Mediterranean main.

## Directions

1.  **Toast:** Heat olive oil and 1 tbsp butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the pearl couscous and stir constantly for 2-3 minutes until the grains are golden brown and fragrant. They should smell like toasted nuts.
2.  **Aromatics:** Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant. Do not let it brown.
3.  **Simmer:** Pour in the broth -- it will sizzle. Add salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 10-12 minutes until the couscous is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Check at 8 minutes -- pearl couscous can go from perfect to mushy quickly.
4.  **Finish:** Remove from heat. Add the remaining 1 tbsp butter, parsley, and lemon zest. Fluff with a fork, stirring until the butter melts and coats every grain.
5.  **Serve:** Taste for salt. Serve warm as a bed for grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or alongside saucy dishes.
