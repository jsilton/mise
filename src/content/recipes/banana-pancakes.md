## Chef's Note

These are real pancakes with real batter -- not the two-ingredient banana-egg variety that falls apart on the griddle. Mashing ripe bananas into a proper buttermilk batter gives you fluffy, tender pancakes with unmistakable banana flavor throughout. The key to height and fluffiness is not overmixing: lumps in the batter are fine and even desirable. Overmixing activates gluten, which makes pancakes tough and flat. Let the batter rest for 5 minutes after mixing so the baking powder can start working.

## Directions

1.  **Mix Dry:** In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
2.  **Mix Wet:** In a separate bowl, combine buttermilk, mashed bananas, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla. Stir until just combined.
3.  **Combine:** Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Fold gently with a spatula until just combined -- the batter should be lumpy with a few streaks of flour still visible. Do not whisk. Let the batter rest for 5 minutes.
4.  **Heat the Griddle:** Heat a griddle or large nonstick skillet over medium heat (325 degrees F if using an electric griddle). Lightly grease with butter. Test with a drop of water -- it should sizzle and evaporate within a second.
5.  **Cook:** Pour 1/3 cup batter per pancake. Cook until the surface is covered in bubbles that begin to pop and the edges look set, about 2-3 minutes. Flip and cook for 1-2 minutes more until golden brown on the bottom and cooked through.
6.  **Serve:** Stack pancakes and top with sliced bananas, a pat of butter, and warm maple syrup. Leftover pancakes freeze well -- cool completely, layer between parchment paper, and store in a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Reheat in a toaster.
