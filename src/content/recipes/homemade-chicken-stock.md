## Chef's Note

A great chicken stock is all about temperature control. Never let the stock boil violently after the initial heating step; high heat emulsifies the chicken fat into the water, resulting in a greasy, cloudy broth. Instead, maintain a gentle bubble (a "smile" on the surface) to extract gelatin from the bones while keeping the stock clean and clear. Leaving the onion skins on adds a beautiful, rich amber color to the finished stock.

## Directions

1.  **Prep:** Place the chicken bones, onion quarters, carrots, celery, and halved garlic bulb in a large 8-to-12-quart stockpot.
2.  **Boil:** Pour in the cold water. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce the heat to low to maintain a gentle simmer.
3.  **Skim:** Skim off any gray foam or impurities that rise to the surface during the first 30 minutes of cooking. This ensures a clean flavor.
4.  **Infuse:** Add the parsley stems, thyme sprigs, bay leaf, and whole black peppercorns to the pot.
5.  **Simmer:** Simmer gently, uncovered, for 4 hours. Keep the heat low so the liquid does not boil. If the water level drops too low and exposes the bones, add a cup or two of hot water.
6.  **Strain:** Pour the stock through a colander lined with cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a large heatproof bowl. Discard the spent bones and vegetables.
7.  **Chill:** Place the bowl of stock in an ice bath to cool it rapidly. Once cool, cover and refrigerate overnight.
8.  **De-fat:** Scrape off the layer of solidified fat from the surface of the chilled stock before using or freezing.
