## Chef's Note

Jerk is a Maroon cooking tradition from Jamaica's Blue Mountains, developed by escaped enslaved Africans who cooked over pimento (allspice) wood to preserve meat in the island's heat. The marinade is defined by two non-negotiables: fresh allspice and scotch bonnet — substitute either and it stops being jerk. For a weeknight reheat, slow-roasting the shoulder until it shreds gives you jerk flavor without the pit, and the meat actually improves after a night in its own juices.

## Directions

1. **Make the marinade:** Combine scallions, peppers, onion, garlic, ginger, thyme, allspice, brown sugar, salt, pepper, cinnamon, nutmeg, soy sauce, lime juice, vinegar, and oil in a blender. Blend until mostly smooth — some texture is fine. Taste: it should be aggressively spiced, salty, and sharp. Reserve 1/4 cup for finishing.
2. **Marinate:** Score the pork shoulder in a shallow cross-hatch 1/4 inch deep. Rub the remaining marinade all over, working into the scored surface. Bag or cover and refrigerate **at least 8 hours, ideally 24**. Longer is better — the allspice and lime need time to penetrate.
3. **Preheat:** 325°F. Bring pork to room temperature for 30 minutes while oven heats.
4. **Sear:** Heat a dutch oven over medium-high until very hot. Scrape excess marinade off the pork (reserve it) and sear all sides until deeply browned, 3-4 minutes per side. Don't rush — the crust is where the bark comes from.
5. **Braise:** Pour the reserved scraped marinade, chicken stock, and orange juice around (not over) the pork. Cover tightly and transfer to oven. Braise for **3 to 3.5 hours**, until the pork shreds easily with a fork.
6. **Rest and shred:** Transfer pork to a board and rest 20 minutes. Skim fat off the braising liquid and reduce on the stovetop for 10 minutes until glossy. Shred the pork with two forks, discarding any large pockets of fat.
7. **Finish:** Toss the shredded pork with the reduced braising liquid and the reserved 1/4 cup fresh marinade. This last spoonful of raw marinade is what keeps the jerk flavor bright after reheating — cooked jerk alone goes dull.
8. **Reheat (day 2 or 3):** Spread in a baking dish, cover with foil, and warm at 325°F for 20-25 minutes. Uncover for the last 5 minutes to re-crisp the edges. A squeeze of fresh lime at the table brings it back to life.
