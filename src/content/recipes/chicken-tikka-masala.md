## Chef's Note

This is the ultimate home version of the world's most popular curry. It strikes a perfect balance between the smoky, charred edges of the chicken and a velvety, spice-rich tomato sauce. The secret to its "restaurant" soul is the Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek), which provides that unmistakable savory aroma that lingers in the air of the best curry houses. It is a technical dish that rewards the extra effort of blooming spices and simmering with a deep, complex comfort.

## Directions

1.  **The Marinade:** In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, garlic, ginger, lemon juice, salt, Kashmiri chili, and Garam Masala. Add the chicken chunks and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, but ideally **overnight** for the most tender results.
2.  **The Char (Broil):** Preheat your oven broiler to HIGH with a rack 5 - 6 inches below the element. Shake off excess marinade and spread the chicken in a single layer (not touching) on a wire rack set over a foil-lined baking sheet. For **thighs**, broil 10 - 12 minutes until you see charred, blackened spots; do not flip. For **breasts** (leaner, dry out fast), broil only 6 - 8 minutes and pull them as soon as the edges char — they should still look slightly underdone in the center. Either way the chicken is only ~75 - 80% cooked here; it finishes in the sauce. If your broiler runs hot, flip once at the halfway mark to keep breasts from scorching before they char.
3.  **The Base:** Heat the butter and oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the onions with a pinch of salt until they are a deep, golden brown (10 - 15 minutes). This "low and slow" onion cook is the foundation of the gravy's body.
4.  **The Bloom:** Add the garlic, ginger, and tomato paste. Stir constantly for 2 minutes until the paste turns a dark brick-red. Reduce heat to low and add the coriander, cumin, turmeric, and paprika. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant - do not let the spices burn.
5.  **The Simmer:** Pour in the tomato passata, the white sugar, and 1 tsp salt. Simmer gently for 15 minutes. The sauce should thicken and darken slightly, and the oil may pool at the edges - a sign the base is ready. For a professional, silky texture, you can blend the sauce now with an immersion blender, but it is not strictly necessary.
6.  **The Finish:** Stir in the heavy cream and the broiled chicken (plus any juices from the tray). Simmer on low until the chicken is just cooked through and tender - about 5 - 8 minutes for thighs, **only 4 - 5 minutes for breasts** (pull them as soon as they hit 165°F; lean breast turns chalky if it overcooks in the sauce). If the gravy gets too thick, loosen it with a splash of water.
7.  **Taste & Adjust:** Off the heat, taste the sauce. Add salt until the flavors pop (it usually needs another ½ tsp), a pinch more sugar if the tomato tastes sharp, and a squeeze of lemon if it feels heavy. This step is what separates a flat curry from a great one.
8.  **The Magic:** Stir in the cold butter for a glossy finish. Crush the Kasuri Methi between your palms to release its oils and sprinkle it over the top along with the final teaspoon of Garam Masala. Rest 5 minutes before serving so the spices settle.
