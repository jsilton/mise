## Chef's Note

Japanese curry is sweeter and less spiced than Indian curry - it comes in a roux base you can buy at any Asian market, but homemade tastes fresher. Katsu curry is the beloved comfort dish where a breaded fried chicken cutlet sits under silky curry sauce with soft vegetables. Japanese home cooks have been making this on weeknights for decades because it's fast, kids love it, and the crispy-creamy contrast is perfect. The key to crispy katsu: pound the chicken thin, dip quickly in the egg wash (not too long or it gets soggy), and fry in oil that's hot enough to sizzle immediately when the chicken hits the pan.

## Directions

1. **Pound the Chicken:** Place each chicken breast on a cutting board. Using a meat mallet or the flat side of a knife handle, pound gently from the center outward until the breast is an even 1/2-inch thickness. Season both sides with salt and pepper.

2. **Set Up Breading Station:** Place flour in one shallow bowl, beaten eggs in another, and panko in a third. Working with one chicken breast at a time, coat lightly in flour, shaking off excess. Dip into egg wash briefly (2 seconds per side), then press into panko, coating both sides thoroughly. Place on a plate and let rest for 2 minutes.

3. **Heat Oil:** In a large skillet, heat 1/2 cup vegetable oil over medium-high heat until the surface shimmers and a breadcrumb dropped in sizzles immediately (about 350°F). This should take 3-4 minutes.

4. **Fry the Katsu:** Working in batches of 2 to avoid crowding, carefully place each breaded chicken breast in the hot oil. Fry for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through (165°F internal temperature). Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Keep warm.

5. **Make the Curry Base:** While the chicken cooks, heat butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Stir in curry powder and tomato paste, cooking for 1 minute to bloom the spices - the mixture should smell sweet and warm, not raw.

6. **Build the Sauce:** Add chicken stock, soy sauce, and mirin. Stir well to combine, breaking up any lumps. Bring to a gentle simmer. Add carrot and potato, and simmer for 8-10 minutes until the vegetables are nearly tender. Add peas and cook for 2 more minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper - the sauce should be balanced between sweet and savory.

7. **Plate:** Place a mound of warm rice on each plate, top with a piece of katsu, and ladle curry sauce (with vegetables) around it. The sauce should pool slightly without drowning the crispy coating.

**Breading Note:** If the egg wash sits on the chicken too long, the coating gets soggy and won't crisp. Work quickly - 2 seconds per side in the egg is plenty.

**Oil Temperature:** If the oil isn't hot enough, the chicken will absorb oil and become greasy instead of crispy. You want an immediate, vigorous sizzle.
