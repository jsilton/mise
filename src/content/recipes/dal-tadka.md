## Chef's Note

Dal tadka is two separate components that come together at the very end: the soft, comforting lentils and the aromatic tempered spice oil (the tadka). The tadka is where the magic happens -- ghee heated until shimmering, then hit with whole spices that crackle and pop in seconds. Pour the tadka over the dal right before serving; the sizzle and fragrance are part of the experience. Do not skip rinsing the lentils until the water runs clear, or your dal will be starchy and gummy.

## Directions

1.  **Cook the Lentils:** In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed lentils, 3 cups water, turmeric, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook uncovered for 20-25 minutes, skimming any foam, until the lentils are completely soft and starting to break down. Stir in the diced tomato and green chiles. Simmer for 5 more minutes. Using a whisk or the back of a spoon, mash some of the lentils to create a creamy texture with some whole lentils remaining.

2.  **Prepare the Tadka:** In a small skillet, heat the ghee over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the cumin seeds and mustard seeds. When they begin to pop and splutter (about 15 seconds), immediately add the curry leaves (careful, they will splatter), dried red chiles, and asafoetida. Stir for 5 seconds.

3.  **Build the Aromatics:** Add the sliced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until golden. Add the diced onion and cook for 4-5 minutes until softened and lightly browned. Stir in the red chile powder and remove from heat immediately.

4.  **Combine:** Pour the hot tadka directly over the simmering dal. You should hear a satisfying sizzle. Stir to combine. Add lemon juice and adjust salt to taste.

5.  **Serve:** Garnish generously with fresh cilantro. Serve hot over basmati rice or with warm naan for scooping.
