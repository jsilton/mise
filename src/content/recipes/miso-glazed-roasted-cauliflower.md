## Chef's Note

Miso is umami condensed - it transforms mild cauliflower into something deeply savory and addictive. The glaze contains the holy trifecta: miso (salt and depth), mirin (sweetness and shine), and sesame oil (richness). Roasting at high heat caramelizes the glaze onto the florets. This is how Japanese home cooks make vegetables disappear from the plate. The key is not overcrowding the pan; each piece needs direct contact with heat to caramelize.

## Directions

1. **Make Glaze:** In a small bowl, whisk together miso, mirin, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and fresh ginger. Taste - it should taste aggressively salty and umami-rich (the cauliflower will mellow it).
2. **Prep Oven:** Preheat to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
3. **Toss:** Pat cauliflower florets dry (moisture prevents caramelization). Place in a large bowl. Pour the miso glaze over and toss with your hands until every floret is coated.
4. **Spread:** Arrange florets in a single layer on the baking sheet, cut-side down. Do not crowd.
5. **Roast:** Bake for 25-30 minutes until the cauliflower is tender inside and the glaze is caramelized and slightly charred on the edges. Shake the pan halfway through.
6. **Finish:** Remove from oven. Garnish generously with sesame seeds and thinly sliced scallions. Serve hot.
