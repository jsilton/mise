## Chef's Note

This is essentially a deconstructed bagel and lox on better bread. Sourdough provides a sturdier, tangier base than a bagel, and toasting it until deeply golden means it can support the weight of the toppings without going limp. Softened cream cheese is essential -- cold cream cheese tears the bread apart when you try to spread it. The acid from capers and lemon juice cuts through the richness of the salmon and cream cheese, and the red onion adds a sharp bite. Keep the salmon in wide, draped folds rather than laying it flat so you get texture in every bite.

## Directions

1.  **Toast:** Toast the bread until deeply golden and structurally rigid -- it should not bend when held horizontally.
2.  **Spread:** Spread a generous layer of softened cream cheese across each toast, edge to edge.
3.  **Layer the Salmon:** Drape the smoked salmon over the cream cheese in loose, ruffled folds -- do not lay it flat. This creates more surface area and better texture.
4.  **Top:** Scatter capers and thinly sliced red onion over the salmon. Squeeze lemon juice directly over the top. Add a few grinds of black pepper and fresh dill fronds.
5.  **Finish:** Sprinkle with everything bagel seasoning if desired.
6.  **Serve:** Eat immediately with a knife and fork. The combination of textures and temperatures is best within the first few minutes.
