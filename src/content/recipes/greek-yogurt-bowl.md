## Chef's Note

A yogurt bowl is only as good as its foundation. Full-fat Greek yogurt provides the thick, tangy, protein-rich base that holds toppings without becoming soupy -- low-fat versions are thinner and less satisfying. The layering order matters: yogurt first, then fruit, then granola last so it stays crunchy. Adding honey and chia seeds at the end ensures the honey drizzles visibly across the surface and the chia seeds maintain their texture rather than absorbing moisture and turning gelatinous.

## Directions

1.  **Build the Base:** Spoon Greek yogurt into a bowl, smoothing it into an even layer.
2.  **Add Fruit:** Arrange fresh berries and banana slices (if using) over the yogurt. Scatter them rather than piling them in the center for even distribution in every bite.
3.  **Add Crunch:** Sprinkle granola, nuts, and chia seeds over the fruit. Keep the granola toward the top so it stays crisp.
4.  **Drizzle:** Finish with a generous drizzle of honey or maple syrup across the entire surface.
5.  **Serve:** Eat immediately. The granola begins absorbing moisture within a few minutes, and the goal is the contrast between creamy yogurt, juicy fruit, and crunchy toppings.
