## Chef's Note

This is the real horiatiki -- the village salad you get at a taverna in the Greek islands, not the American diner version loaded with lettuce and croutons. There is no lettuce. The feta is a thick slab on top, not crumbled. The olives are unpitted. The vegetables are cut large and rustic, not diced. Every component should be at room temperature to maximize flavor. Use the best olive oil you own because you will taste it in every bite. This salad lives and dies by the quality of its tomatoes.

## Directions

1.  **Prep the Vegetables:** Cut the tomatoes into large irregular chunks -- they should be rustic, not precise. Halve the cucumber lengthwise, then cut into thick half-moons. Slice the bell pepper into thick rings. Slice the red onion into thin half-rings and separate them.

2.  **Assemble:** Arrange the tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, and red onion on a wide, shallow platter or bowl. Scatter the olives and capers (if using) throughout.

3.  **The Feta:** Place the block of feta in one piece directly on top of the vegetables. Sprinkle the dried oregano generously over the feta slab and the vegetables.

4.  **Dress:** Drizzle the olive oil generously over everything, then splash the red wine vinegar over the top. Sprinkle with salt.

5.  **Serve:** Serve immediately at room temperature. Each person breaks off feta as they serve themselves. Do not refrigerate before serving -- cold tomatoes have no flavor.
