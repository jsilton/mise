## Chef's Note

Saag means leafy greens, and in Punjabi homes the dish is rustic and textured - the greens are chopped and fried into a spiced masala rather than blended into the smooth purée you get in a restaurant's palak paneer. The single most important step is squeezing the cooked spinach bone-dry; watery greens make a muddy, gray sauce instead of a glossy one that clings to each cube. And don't rush the paneer - slipping the browned cubes into hot, salted water keeps them pillowy instead of rubbery.

## Directions

1.  **Soak the Paneer (do this first):** If your paneer isn't already browned, fry the cubes in 1 tbsp ghee over medium-high until golden on most sides. Either way, slip the cubes into a bowl of hot water with a big pinch of salt and leave them there until step 6 - this is what keeps fried paneer soft.
2.  **Dry the Spinach:** Blanch fresh spinach in salted boiling water for about 1 minute, then drain and shock in ice water to lock in the green. Chop to roughly 1/4-inch and squeeze it bone-dry in a towel until almost no liquid runs out. (For frozen spinach, thaw and squeeze hard.) _Optional binder: blend a small handful into a paste and keep the rest chunky for a sauce that holds together but still has chew._
3.  **Bloom the Cumin:** Heat 2 tbsp ghee in a large skillet over medium. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle and pop for 30 seconds until fragrant.
4.  **Build the Masala:** Add the onion and cook 4-5 minutes until soft and golden at the edges. Add the ginger, garlic, and jalapeño; fry 1-2 minutes. Stir in the turmeric and coriander and toast 30 seconds until aromatic.
5.  **Fry the Greens:** Add the chopped spinach (and the blended paste, if using) plus the salt. **Stir vigorously to coat every bit in the spiced fat**, then cook 3-4 minutes. This frying step - not a long simmer - is where textured saag gets its flavor. Add a splash of water only if it sticks.
6.  **Cream and Finish:** Stir in the cream and let it bubble 1-2 minutes to tighten around the greens (add more if it looks dry). Crush in the kasuri methi. Drain the soaked paneer and fold it in gently; warm through 2 minutes - don't stir hard or the paneer will break. Off the heat, stir in the garam masala and a squeeze of lemon. Taste and adjust salt.
7.  **Optional Tadka:** Bloom 1 tbsp ghee with the Kashmiri chili until it sizzles, then pour over the top. Spoon it only over the adults' portions to keep the base mild for kids.

**For smooth palak-style:** Purée all of the blanched spinach with 1/4 cup water instead of chopping, add it in step 5, and simmer gently rather than frying - everything else stays the same.

**Make-Ahead:** Keeps 3-4 days refrigerated. Reheat gently with a splash of cream; hard reheating toughens the paneer.
