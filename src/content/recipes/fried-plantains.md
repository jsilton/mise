## Chef's Note

Maduros are the sweet cousin of tostones — ripe plantains pan-fried until the sugars caramelize into a glossy, jammy interior with crisp edges. The single thing that matters is ripeness: the plantains should look nearly ruined, with skins that are **mostly black with yellow patches**. Yellow plantains are too starchy and won't caramelize — buy them four or five days before you plan to cook them.

## Directions

1. **Check ripeness:** The plantains should yield to gentle pressure and the skin should be mostly black. If still firm and yellow, leave them on the counter another day or two.
2. **Peel and slice:** Score the skin end-to-end with a knife, peel back (easier than a banana once ripe). Slice on a sharp diagonal into 1/2-inch pieces.
3. **Heat the pan:** Add oil to a large skillet over medium heat. When it shimmers, add plantain slices in a single layer — don't crowd, or they steam instead of caramelize.
4. **Fry:** Cook **3-4 minutes per side**, until deeply golden and the sugars have caramelized into a sticky crust. Flip once — they're delicate when soft.
5. **Drain and season:** Transfer to a paper-towel-lined plate. Sprinkle with a small pinch of salt (it amplifies the sweetness). A dusting of cinnamon is optional.
6. **Serve immediately:** Maduros lose their magic as they cool — crisp edges soften and the caramelization dulls. Plate straight from the pan.
