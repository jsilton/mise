## Meal Overview

A fresh, nutritious, and child-friendly poke bowl dinner portioned for two adults and one child. Cold cubed Ahi tuna is dressed in a savory, ginger-kissed shoyu sauce and served over seasoned sushi rice with sliced cucumbers, edamame, and avocado.

## Cooking Strategy

1. **Cook and season the rice:** Cook Japanese short-grain rice and gently fold in the warm vinegar-sugar-salt seasoning (sushi-zu). Cover with a damp cloth to keep lukewarm.
2. **Prep the toppings:** Thinly slice the cucumber, dice the avocado, and thaw the edamame.
3. **Whisk the dressing:** Mix the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, grated ginger, sugar, and scallions in a medium bowl.
4. **Marinate the tuna:** Cube the cold tuna, gently fold it into the dressing, and refrigerate for exactly 10 to 15 minutes before serving (timing is key to prevent curing).
5. **Assemble & Garnish:** Scoop the seasoned lukewarm rice into bowls, top with the marinated cold tuna, arrange the cucumber, avocado, and edamame, and garnish with sesame seeds. Serve sriracha mayo on the side for adults.
