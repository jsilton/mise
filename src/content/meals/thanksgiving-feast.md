## Meal Overview

The ultimate Thanksgiving feast featuring a classic roast turkey with crispy skin and juicy meat, accompanied by a rich sausage and kale stuffing, velvety mashed potatoes, citrusy cranberry-orange sauce, and bright green beans. This menu is designed to serve a crowd and deliver the quintessential holiday dinner experience.

## Cooking Strategy

1. **2 Days Ahead: Brine the turkey** - Clean the bird and immerse it in the aromatic brine. Keep refrigerated.
2. **1 Day Ahead: Cranberry Sauce & Prep Veggies** - Cook the cranberry-orange sauce (it tastes better after sitting). Chop your onions, celery, and kale for the stuffing.
3. **Morning of: Prep Stuffing & Turkey** - Take the turkey out of the brine, rinse, pat dry, and let air-dry in the fridge for extra crispy skin. Toast the bread cubes for stuffing.
4. **5 hours before: Roast the Turkey** - Roast according to weight (approx. 15 mins per pound at 325°F/160°C).
5. **2.5 hours before: Assemble & Bake Stuffing** - Combine stuffing ingredients and bake alongside the turkey.
6. **1.5 hours before: Potatoes** - Boil and mash the potatoes, keeping them warm in a covered bowl or slow cooker.
7. **30 mins before: Rest Turkey & Make Gravy** - Remove turkey from the oven to rest (crucial for juiciness). Pour off pan drippings to make turkey gravy.
8. **15 mins before: Sauté Green Beans** - Quick-cook the green beans in a pan with shallots and lemon juice so they stay vibrant.
9. **Carve & Feast!**

## Notes

- Make sure to save the turkey carcass and neck to make a rich [Homemade Chicken Stock](/mise/recipes/homemade-chicken-stock).
- Ensure the turkey reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) in the thickest part of the thigh before carving.
