## Meal Overview

A Southern-American comfort spread that works on a weeknight because the pulled pork does the heavy lifting  -  it's either been slow-cooked over the weekend or from a batch in the freezer. Savory cornbread with low sugar to complement (not compete with) the sweet-smoky bark. Steamed broccoli for greens, elote-style corn for the kids' favorite. The whole spread hits the table in 25 minutes once the pork is ready.

## Cooking Strategy

1. **Reheat the pork**  -  if from the fridge or freezer, warm in a covered pan with a splash of stock over medium-low heat
2. **Start the cornbread**  -  mix and get it into the oven at 400°F (20-25 min bake)
3. **While cornbread bakes**  -  steam the broccoli (5-6 min) and prep the corn (grill or broil, 10-15 min)
4. **Plate family-style**  -  let everyone build their own plate

## Notes

- The pulled pork is the make-ahead star here  -  cook on Sunday, eat all week (sandwiches, tacos, rice bowls)
- Cornbread is deliberately savory with only 2 tbsp brown sugar + 2 tbsp honey  -  it's a BBQ companion, not a dessert
- Kids love the corn on the cob  -  let them skip the chili powder if they want
- Leftover cornbread + pulled pork = school lunch next day
