## Meal Overview

A rich, savory, and caramelized stir-fry dinner paired with a crisp, refreshing, and acidic salad, portioned for four adults and one child. Chewy, hot-soaked rice noodles are stir-fried with chicken thighs and Chinese broccoli (Gai Lan) in a sweet dark soy sauce. Smashed Cucumber Salad provides a crunchy, bright palate cleanser between bites of the noodles.

## Cooking Strategy

1. **Soak the noodles:** Place dried XL rice noodles in hot water for 15 to 25 minutes until pliable. Drain and toss immediately with 1 tbsp neutral oil.
2. **Make the cucumber salad:** Smash, slice, and dress the cucumbers. Place in the refrigerator to chill and marinate (at least 10 minutes).
3. **Prep the stir-fry components:** Slice the chicken thighs. Mix the Pad See Ew sauce ingredients. Cut the Chinese broccoli (Gai Lan), keeping the sliced stems separate from the leaves. Mince the garlic.
4. **Sear the chicken & stems:** Stir-fry the chicken and Gai Lan stems in a smoking-hot wok until cooked through (about 6 minutes). Set aside on a plate.
5. **Steam-sear the noodles:** Add the noodles to the wok, let them char for 30 seconds, then pour the sauce over them. Splash in a couple tablespoons of water to create steam and finish cooking. Toss to coat.
6. **Scramble the eggs:** Push the noodles to one side of the wok, scramble the eggs in the empty space, and fold them in.
7. **Assemble and Wilt:** Return the chicken, stems, and raw Gai Lan leaves to the wok, tossing for 60 seconds until the leaves wilt.
8. **Serve:** Portion the Pad See Ew into plates and serve immediately with the ice-cold Smashed Cucumber Salad.
