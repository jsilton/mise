## Meal Overview

A warm, fragrant Moroccan plate where the tagine does most of the work ahead of time. Bone-in chicken braises with chickpeas, apricots, preserved lemon, and warm North African spice — the kind of dish that tastes more like itself on day two. Pearl couscous is the starch that holds up to reheating better than fine-grain Moroccan couscous, and the cold carrot salad brings citrus brightness and crunch to cut through the sweet-savory braise.

## Cooking Strategy

**Saturday or Monday (make-ahead day, about 55 min active):**

1. Mix the spice blend. Season and sear the chicken thighs skin-side down until deep golden — 6-8 minutes.
2. Caramelize onions 10-12 minutes. Bloom spices, add tomato paste, deglaze with stock.
3. Add chickpeas, apricots, preserved lemon, saffron, cinnamon stick, honey. Nestle chicken back in.
4. Cover and simmer 45-50 min on low; uncover last 10 min.
5. Cool completely before refrigerating. Don't toast the almonds yet.

**Tuesday or Thursday (reheat day — 25 minutes):**

1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Transfer tagine to a baking dish with a splash of water or stock. Cover with foil and reheat 20-25 minutes, uncovering the last 5 to re-crisp the chicken skin.
2. Cook pearl couscous fresh: saute garlic in butter, toast couscous, add broth, simmer 15 minutes. Finish with parsley and lemon zest.
3. Grate carrots and dress the salad — let sit 15-30 minutes while the tagine reheats.
4. Toast slivered almonds in a dry pan, 3-4 minutes. Chop herbs.
5. Plate: spoon couscous on one side, tagine with sauce next to it. Scatter toasted almonds and fresh herbs over the chicken. Carrot salad alongside.

## Make-Ahead Notes

- **Tagine improves substantially overnight.** The apricots plump, the preserved lemon mellows, the chicken soaks in the sauce. Friday-night lamb tagine for Sunday dinner is a classic Moroccan move for exactly this reason.
- **Don't toast the almonds until serving.** They go soft in the fridge and lose the crunch that contrasts the braised chicken.
- **Pearl couscous can be made 2 days ahead** — reheat with a splash of water, covered. It reheats better than fine couscous.
- **Carrot salad holds 2 days** in the fridge; mint and parsley are added at serving.
- **A wedge of lemon at the table** revives reheated tagine the same way lime revives jerk.

## Kid Notes

- Omit cayenne from the spice blend for a fully mild version — the allspice, cinnamon, and paprika carry plenty of warmth without heat.
- Kids tend to love the apricots and chickpeas; some will pick around the preserved lemon (which is fine — it's there for perfume and saltiness, not to be eaten whole).
- The carrot salad is sweet and bright; if orange blossom water isn't a hit, skip it — still delicious without.

## Notes

- If you can't find preserved lemon, the closest substitute is zest of 1 lemon + 2 tsp capers + a pinch of extra salt. Not identical, but it gets you the briny note.
- A real tagine (conical earthenware pot) is beautiful but not required — a dutch oven with a tight lid works exactly as well.
- Leftover tagine with a fried egg on top is one of the great breakfast hacks.
